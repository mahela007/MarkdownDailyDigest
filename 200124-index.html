Plasmodium falciparum invades human erythrocytes by redundant pathways.

The life cycles of P. vivax and P. ovale include a dormant hepatic stage called the hypnozoite.
Dormant hypnozoites don't cause symptoms and cannot be detected by any tests

HLA molecules

HLA molecules are glycoproteins found on cell surfaces which are used for antigen presentation.
They are encoded by the Major histocompatibility complex region of genes on chromosome 6.
(for context, MHC gene clusters exist on many species, like mice for example. In humans, the MHC genes are called human leukocyte antigen HLA genes)

There are 6 types of HLA molecules.

  • HLA A , B and C are class I HLA molecules.
    • They are used to present proteins found within the cell to the extracellular space.
  • HLA DP, DQ and DR are class II HLA molecules.
    • They are used to present antigens obtained from outside the cell
