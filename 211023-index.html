Parvovirus B19

#2020SBR-JUL/Q16

Benign to life threatening manifestations.

[!TIP] Erythropoiesis seems to be more affected
Although parvovirus B19 causes aplastic anaemia, the description suggests that in general, pure red cell aplasis is more common.

5 recognized clinical syndromes:

  1. Fifth disease: "erythema infectiosum" in children
    1. Fever, coryza, headache and diarrhoea followed by 'slapped cheek' rash
  2. Arthropathy
    1. Acute onset, symmetric arthritis affecting small joints of the hands, wrists, knees and feet.
  3. Transient aplastic crisis in those with chronic haemolytic disorders
    1. Transient 'suspension of erythropoisis' -> severe anaemia.
    2. Common in sickle cell disease.
    3. Usually manifests are pure red cell asplasia but WBC and platelets can also reduce.
  4. Fetal infection leading to non-immune hydrops fetalis and fetal death
  5. Pure red cell aplasia in immunocompromised individuals

Chronic infection in immunosuppressed hosts manifests as a pure red cell aplasia.

Methotrexate toxicity

#2020SBR-JUL/Q16

[!TIP]

  • High dose therapy can case myelosuppression, liver toxicity and renal toxicity and lung toxicity. See high dose toxicity below.
  • Low dose therapy can cause GI side effects, rash on elbows and knees, alopecia and lung toxicity and macrocytosis (or isolated ⬇Hb, ⬇PLT or ⬇WBC)

[!INFO] Toxicity is precipitated by renal impairment
MTX is renally excreted so renal impairment causes drug accumulation.

Most probable side effects depend on whether therapy is low dose or high dose.
Weakly doses, used in Rheumatoid Arthritis are considered low doses.

  • Nausea, vomiting and diarrhoea
  • Rash - particularly on elbow and knees
  • Alopecia
  • Soreness of the mouth / stomatitis
  • Macrocytosis - seen with low dose therapy.
  • Hepatoxicity - due to direct drug effect OR enhancent of viral damage in patients with hepatitis.
    • screen for hepatitis before beginning therapy
  • Pulmonary toxicity
    • Seen with low and high dose therapy
    • Acute or chronic presentation
    • MTX induced pneumonitis or infection can cause lung symptoms.
    • Usually occurs within the first year of treatment. (i.e not something that happens with prolonged therapy)
  • Nephrotoxicity: Very rare with low dose regiments. Possible with high doses.
  • Myelosuppression
    • Infrequent with low doses
    • Is the major dose limiting side effect in HIGH DOSE MTX therapy.
    • Low dose therapy (used for RA) usually causes macrocytosis.
    • but pancytopenia is a more serious complication
    • Therefore, FBC must be measured every 4 weeks for the first 3 months of therapy and then every 8 to 12 weeks.
      • Mnemonic: every month for 3 months and then every 2 - 3 months
  • Teratogenic / abortifacient in #pregnancy
High dose toxicity

High dose therapy is therapy > 500mg/m2 used in CNS prophylaxis of leukemia and high risk lymphoma.

90% of MTX is excreted unchanged in urine; it can precipitate in tubules (esp. acidic urine) to cause AKI. Urinary alkalinization is used in treatment.
