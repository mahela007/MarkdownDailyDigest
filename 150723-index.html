Megaloblastic anaemia

Megaloblasts present in bone marrow.

Causes

Occurs due to a defect in DNA synthesis => which in turn can be caused by

  1. B12 deficiency
  2. Folate deficiency
  3. Drugs intefering with DNA synthesis
  4. metabolic errors of DNA synthesis

Basis of folate and B12 deficiency causing megaloblastic anaemia

  1. Folate is converted to methylene THF which is required for thymidine synthesis -> which is needed to make DNA
    1. When methylene THF is used for this purpose, it is converted to methyl-THF. This is unusable.
  2. (Dietary folate is one source of tetrahydrofolate in the folate cycle)
  3. The methyl THF is recycled to THF so that it can be reconverted to methylene THF and more DNA can be made.
  4. The recycling is requires action of the methionine synthase reaction for which B12 is a cofactor.

Deficiency of B12 and Folate both result in deficiency of methylene THF and therefore, defiency of DNA sythesis -> megaloblastic anaemia

B12 metabolism
