Effects of Noradrenaline on kidneys

  • Increased sodium reabsorption (direct action on tubules)

Regulation Of Sodium reabsorption

[!INFO] Sodium is the most important determinant of plasma osmolality

Variations in Na Excretion are produced by

  1. changes in GFR (i.e how much sodium is filtered)
  2. How much is reabsorbed (mainly by adjusting the 3% of reabsorption in the collecting ducts)

GFR is regulated by factors such as tubuloglomerular feed back.

  • Tubuloglomerular feeback - low flow in the TAL and DCT triggers increased GFR and vice versa. [[#^c5fd5c]]
    • In tubuloglomerular feedback, the macula densa cells of the DCT sense an increase in the sodium they receive via the tubule and send signals to the afferent arteriolar smooth muscle (to cause vasocontriction of AE) and the juxtaglomerular cells (to reduce renin secretion)
  • Glomerulotubular balance is the converse of this:
    • Increased GFR causes increased solute reabsorption in the PCT (mainly Na)
    • so that a constant proportion of the filteres Na is reabsorbed.

Reabsorption is regulated by

  1. level of aldosterone (exerts effects within 30 minutes)
  2. other adrenocortical hormones.
  3. ANP inhibits ENaC mediated Na resorption -> natriuresis
  4. PGE -> causes natriuresis by inhibiting Na/K ATPAse
  5. Endothelin and IL-1 also cause natriuresis
  6. Angiotensin acts directly on the proximal tubules to and bicarconate HCO3
  7. The rate of tubular secretion of H+ and K+ affects Na reabsorption.

Metabolic alkalosis

Vomiting and NG suction cause alkalosis because Source

  1. With normal swallowing, HCO3 is secreted in the intestine to neutralize the luminal acid, releasing H+ into the circulation.
  2. This doesn't happen when gastric juice is lost orally.

Metabolic alkalosis is usually accompanied by hypokalemia and hypochloremia.
This reduction in chloride (Cl-) is not accompanied by hyponatremia Source

There are two types of metabolic alkalosis depending on the volume status:
Low ECF volume + alkalosis => Contraction alkalosis.
High ECF Volume + alkalosis => Alkaosis with volume expansion.
Clinical differentiation is with urine chloride.

Contraction alkalosis -> Low urine chloride (< 20mmol/l)
ECF expanstion -> Urine chloride > 20mmol/l.

Aspirin

  • Inhibits COX-1 -> inhibits Thromboxane A2 production
  • Platelet cannot make new enzymes (as they're anucleate)

Dipyridamole

  • Mechanism: inhibits phosphodiesterase, inhibits thromboxane A2 synthesis.
  • Unlike aspirin, it does not increase bleeding risk.

P2Y12 receptor angatonists

  • Clopidogrel and prasugrel irreversibly inhibit the binding of adenosine to P2Y12 receptors
  • Ticagrelow produces non-competitive reversible inhibition.
  • They inhibit adenosine mediated platelet aggregation.
  • Clopidogrel is the prodrug. It's activated by the CYP2C19 enzyme system which can be inhibited by omeprazole!. Don't co administer the drugs.
