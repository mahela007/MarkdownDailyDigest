[!INFO] Definition of acute liver failure
Acute liver failure = (1) Acute liver injury + (2) Hepatic encephalopathy + (3) Incr. INR
ALF has a poor prognosis if untreated.

Causes of acute liver failure -ABCs

Commonest causes in adults: Drug induced and viral
(western countries - commonest is drugs, asia and africa - it's viral hepatitis)

ABCs upto "I"

Letter Cause
A Acetaminophen, hepatitis A, autoimmune hepatitis, Amanita phalloides (mushroom poisoning), adenovirus
B Hepatitis B*, Budd-Chiari syndrome
C Cryptogenic, hepatitis C, CMV
D Hepatitis D, drugs and toxins
E Hepatitis E, EBV
F Fatty infiltration - acute fatty liver of pregnancy, Reye's syndrome
G Genetic - Wilson disease
H Hypoperfusion (ischemic hepatitis, SOS, sepsis), HELLP syndrome, HSV, heat stroke, hepatectomy, HLH -hemophagocytic lymphohistiocytosis
I Infiltration by tumor

* Hepatitis C virus alone does not appear to be a significant cause of acute liver failure in the absence of coinfection with hepatitis B.

[!INFO] Reye Syndrome
Reye syndrome is a rapidly progressive encephalopathy with hepatic dysfunction, which often begins several days after apparent recovery from a viral illness, especially varicella or influenza A or B. Liver has marked steatosis. Salicylate (aspirin) use is a risk factor.
