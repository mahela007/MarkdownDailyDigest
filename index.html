Sulfonylureas

Background

First ones used therapeutically - Tolbutamide. - First generation.
Second generation - glibenclamide, glipizide. - more potent but produced similar degree of blood sugar control to Gen 1.

Mechanism

Block of K+ ATP channels by sulphonylureas -> depolarization of beta cell -> Calcium influx -> Insulin secretion.

Well absorbed after oral administration. All are highly bound to albumin.
[[General Pharmacology#Protein bound drugs]]
Excreted in the urine unchanged or as metabolites: action prolonged in renal disease.
Contraindicated in #pregnancy -> Crosses placenta AND appears in breastmilk.

The protein bound from and free form of drugs are in equilibrium. The bound form acts as a reservoir. The unbound form is active and also subject to elimination.

For most drugs at therapeutic concentration, the carrier proteins are very far from saturated.
