Management of Conn's syndrome

+ mechanisms for hyponatremia in cortisol deficiency are (1) **impaired renal water handling in the absence of circulating cortisol** and (2) increased plasma concentrations of arginine vasopressin (AVP), despite hypo-osmolality [Source](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32097946/#:~:text=The%20underlying%20mechanisms%20for%20the,)%2C%20despite%20hypo%2Dosmolality.)

[!INFO] Is an autoimmume, T cell infiltrative disease.

  • Autoimmunity -> TSH-Receptor antibodies (thyrotropin receptor or TRAb) stimulate thyroid gland to make thyroid hormones. -> diffuse, non tender, goiter. (MCQ Point: TSH receptor inhibiting antibodies are also formed) Source
  • Histology: Diffuse, hyperplasia and hypertrophy;
    • Opthalmopathy is commoner in smokers (UpToDate) #2015MCQ/Q54
  • Orbitopathy and pretibial myxoedema are not related to thyroxine levels. Orbitopathy does not correlate with disease activity. (can occur after treatment of hyperthyroidism)
    • Pretibial myxoedema is caused by deposition of glycosaminoglycans (also known as mucopolysaccharides) and T cell infiltration.
  • Acropachy - a form of clubbing seen in Grave's disease; strongly associated with dermopathy. (visible on X ray)
  • Patients will usually eventually become hypothyroid.

A complex series of events causes orbital fibroblasts to produce glycosaminoglycans which accumulate (also increase osmolarity -> orbital oedema) which cause proptosis.

Management of Grave's disease

  • Management of hyperthyroidism - 12 to 18 months of antithyroid drugs.
    • 70% go into permanent remissions.
    • Can try a second time.
    • If relapse still occurs, radioiodine or surgery.
    • Beta blockers for symptomatic relief while the antithyroid drugs work (takes about 3 months)
  • Management of eye disease:
    • Steroids : oral or IV (preferred if severe inflammation)
    • Teprotumumab - monoclonal antibody. (preferred for proptosis and diplopia)
    • Last line: orbital radiation or orbital decompression surgery.
      | General advantages of PTU: Achieves euthyroid state more quickly, Once daily dosing, less likely to cause failure of radioiodine therapy | Usually second choice except for T1, T storm |
      | Pruritus, rash, arthritis, urticaria, abnormal taste | Same |
  • Typical history: Woman of childbearing age who is overweight, presents with episodes of severe throbbing headache with papilloedema (bilateral and symmetric) and pulsatile tinnitus.
  • They can have visual disturbances and diplopia due to abducens nerve palsy.
  • Inheritance #autosomalDominant
  • Episodes of angiooedema not responding to anti-histamines or adrenaline, usually after a flu like prodrome.
  • Caused by C1 esterase deficiency.
  • No urticaria.
  • Deficiency of C1 esterase causes unchecked activation of C1, C2 and C4. (i.e classical pathway)
    • Serum C4 is the most reliable and widely used screening tool. Low levels of C2 and C4 are seen even between attacks.
    • C1-INH is low only during an attack
