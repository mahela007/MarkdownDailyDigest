Burkitt lymphoma

  • Classicaly presents as cheek swelling.
  • Caused by EBV.
  • Pathogensis: chromosome 8:14 transolcation.
  • Translocation of cMYC gene to IgG heavy chain locus.
  • B cells begin expressing large amounts of growth regulatory protein which results in rapid cell proliferation.
  • Very Rapidly growing cancer.
  • Can be treated effectively with chemo if identified early.
  • Starry sky pattern on histology.
  • CNIII enters the orbit through the superior orbital fissure.

[!INFO] Summary: Toxoplasmosis

  • Usually presents with flu like signs + lymphadenopathy
  • Usually asymptomatic in immunocompetent people. -> No treatment required.
    • Intracerebral mass lesions are common and chorioretinitis can occur.
  • Latent infection persists lifelong even in immunocompetent people.
  • Vertical transmission only occurs if toxo is acquired during pregnancy.

    1. Both oocysts and tissues cysts transform into tachzoites after ingestion which migrate to neural and muscle tissue.
    2. The tissue forms are called bradyzoites.
    3. If a pregnant woman gets infected during pregnancy, the tachyzoites and cross the placenta.
  • Tranmission can rarely occur with organ transplantation.
  • Diagnosis: Main method used is serology: IgG and IgM.
    - High IgG avidity is seen if the infection has been present for more than 4 months.
  • MRI: multiple ring enhancing lesions. (seeing a single lesion on MRI makes toxoplasmosis unlikely)
  • Atypical lymphocytosis is present.
    • Pyrimethamine - first line drug; combined with sulfasalazine or clindamycin. TMP-SMX can also be used.
      • Pyrimethamine is an antiparasitic prescription medicine
