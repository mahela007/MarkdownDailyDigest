Membranoproliferative glomerulonephritis

mixed nephrotic / nephritic picture. => nephrotic Xn + haematuria, hypertension and impaired renal function

Most patients will go on to develop ESKD over several years;
recurs in renal transplant patients.

C3 complement levels are low.
Type 1 <= activation classical complement pathway - low C3 levels, normal C4 levels. (Tramline pattern seen)
Type 2 <= activation of alternative complement pathway

Haemolytic uraemic syndrome HUS

![[HUS clinicalTriad.png]]

  1. Thrombocytopaenia
  2. Intravascular haemolysis -> anaemia (schistocytes on blood picture)
    Diarrhoea (esp. due to Shiga toxin producin E-coli (O157 strain)) can cause HUS.
    Patient has bloody diarrhoea
    Toxin has A and B subunits. B facilitates entry into cells.
    Toxin promotes formation of intravascular thrombi.
    ![[microthrombi in HUS.png]]

Rarely (5%) developd ESKD but 1/3 will have persistent proteinuria after recovery.
Supportive care (but 5% mortality)
Antibiotics for diarrhoea increase the risk of HUS.
ADAMTS13 is NORMAL in HUS but reduced in TTP.
