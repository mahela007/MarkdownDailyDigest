  • HFrEF: Not demonstrably superior to ACE inhibitors but have less incidence of cough and angiooedema.

#2017BSQ-APR/Q02

NSAID mediated kidney injury can occur in several ways.

  1. haemodynamically mediated kidney injury
  2. Electrolyte abnormalities and acid base disorders
    • NSAIDS can cause hyperkalemia, hyponatremia and oedema
    • Hyperkalemia because NSAIDS impair 1) PG mediated renin release and 2) Angiotensin II mediated aldosterone release.
    • Hyponatremia : NSAIDS Inhibit the ADH antagonistic effects of PG. (i.e NSAIDS promote ADH like action)
    • Hypokalemia with renal tubular acidosis
  3. Acute interstitial nephritis [[2020-SBR-NOV#Acute interstitial nephritis|AIN]]
  4. AIN +/- nephrotic syndrome.
  5. Papillary necrosis

Haemodynamically mediated NSAID induced kidney injury

  • Hemodynamically mediated AKI due to NSAIDs occurs via attenuation of PG-mediated renal vasodilation. In healthy patients, PGs play little role in renal hemodynamics.
  • Kidney prostaglandins are primarily local vasodilators.
  • In the setting of hypotension and reduced kidney perfusion from vasoconstriction stimulated by angiotensin II, norepinephrine, vasopressin, or endothelin, PG synthesis is increased to maintain kidney perfusion and minimize ischemia
  • Prostaglandins also increase renin secretion, antagonize the water-retentive effects of ADH, and enhance sodium excretion (See UpToDate "NSAIDs: Electrolyte complications")
    • i.e Prostaglandings will
      • Increase renin secretion
      • Promote diuresis
      • Promote hyponatremia
