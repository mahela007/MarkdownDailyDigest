Complications of EBV infection

#2020BSQ-JUL/Q28
In addition to those listed above,

  • Chronic active EBV infection - Persistent IM like syndrome with continued viraemia. It's a life threatening lymphoproliferative disorder.
  • Associated
    • with multiple sclerosis,
    • oral hairy leukoplakia which is also associated with HIV (it is NOT a premalignant lesion)
    • Lymphoproliferative disorders
      • Hemophagocytic lymphohistiocytosis
      • Post-transplant lymphoproliferative disease
    • Malignancy - proteins made by the virus cause DNA instability
      • lymphomas in transplant recipients
      • Burkitt lymphoma — Burkitt lymphoma (BL), which is characteristically localized in the jaw
      • In HIV patients, EBV is associated with
        • Non-Hodgkin lymphoma
        • Smooth muscle tumours in children
      • Hodgkin lymphoma
      • Nasopharyngeal carcinoma
      • Gastric carcinoma
      • T cell lymphoma

Tay Sach's disease

#2020BSQ-JUL/Q29
A #autosomal-Recessive defect in hexosaminidase which causes accumulation of lipids within cells. Particularly affects CNS neurons. Affected children develop normally until about 6 months of age and then show cognitive decline with loss of milestones, dementia, blindness and chery red spots in the eyes.
There is no treatment.

#2020BSQ-JUL/Q29

  1. [[2022 May Basic Sciences#Chronic granulomatous disease|Chronic Granulomatous disease]] <- a defect in neutrophil killing.
  2. Leukocyte adhesion deficiency -> a defect in 'firm' adhesion of leukocytes to vessel walls caused by defect in integrins. #autosomal-Recessive
    • Presents in early life with delayed umbilical cord separation, failure to lose primary dentition, severe gingivitis. There is neutrophilia but the cells cannot reach sites of infection. Recurrent infections in the first decade of life. Treatment is stem cell transplant.
  3. Hyper IgE syndrome - Job's syndrome
    • #autosomalDominant
    • abnormal T cell function, increased IgE production and intermittent defects in neutrophil chemotaxis.
    • Dermatitis, boils, pneumonia
  4. Schwachman-Diamond syndrome
    • Mild neutropenia + defect in neutrophil migration.
    • Resembles cystic fibrosis clinically with exocrine pancreatic failure + pyogenic infections.
  5. Chediak-Higashi syndrome
    • Defective phagolysosom fusion. (large lysosome vesicles are seen within the neutrophils)
    • Recurrent infections, anaemia, hepatomegaly.
    • One cause of primary haemophagocytic lymphohistiocytosis.

Acquired neutrophil defects / neutropenia

  • Hypersplenism
  • Immunomodulatory drugs: colchicine, MTX
  • Diabetes:
