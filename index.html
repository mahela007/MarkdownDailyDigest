Localization ischemia of ST elevation MI

See: [[2023-SEMPaper#Coronary territories]]

[!INFO] Only ST elevations can be localized
ST depressions cannot be localized. Source

Leads with ST segment elevations Affected myocardial area Occluded coronary artery (cuprit)
V1V2 Septal Proximal LAD.
V3V4 Anterior LAD.
V5V6 Apical Distal LAD, LCx or RCA.
I, aVL Lateral LCx.
II, aVF, III Inferior 90% RCA. 10% LCx.
V7, V8, V9 (reciprocal ST dips. frequently seen in V1V3) Posterolateral (also referred to as inferobasal or posterior) RCA or LCx.
Source

V1-V2 (septal leads): primarily observes the ventricular septum, but may occasionally display ECG changes originating from the right ventricle. Note that none of the leads in the 12-lead ECG are adequate to detect vectors of the right ventricle. Source
V5-V6 - most sources say V5-V6 look at the lateral wall +/- anterior wall. Not the apex as mentioned above.

[!INFO] The septal Q wave
The septum normally depolorizes from left to right; this causes Q waves in V5 and V6.

Supply of the sinoatrial node:

  • In 60%, supplied by the right coronary artery.
  • In 40%, supplied by the left coronary artery.
