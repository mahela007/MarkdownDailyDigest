Pathophysiolgy

The pathophysiology of GBS involves an inflammatory response against either the myelin or the axon of peripheral nerves due to molecular mimicry and cross reactivity with the infectious antigen.

All myelinated nerves are involved : motor, sensory, autonomic and cranial
1. "This form of GBS is distinguished from AIDP by its selective involvement of motor nerves. Sensory nerves are not affected"
1. The most common presenting symptom of MFS is diplopia, which arises due to the acute onset of external ophthalmoplegia. Source
[!INFO] Demyelination vs. Axonal damage

  • In AIDP and Miller Fischer syndreom the inflammation targets the myelin producing Schwann cells. --> demyelination occurs. Lymphocytic infiltration present.
  • In AMAN and AMSAN, immune reactions occur against the axonal membrane. No lymphocytic infiltration.
    [!TIP] Mnemonic: If the name contains 'I', then myelin is involved and infiltration with lymphocytes occurs.

Aetiology of GBS

  • Preceding campylobacter infection - commonest
  • CMV infection
  • Influenza A and B infection
  • others

Time course of symptoms:

  • Patient notice a problem and present within 1 week.
  • Symptoms progress over the next few weeks and peaks at 4 weeks.
  • Subacute progression can take 4 - 8 weeks.
  • If symptom progression takes more than 8 weeks, the dianosis is chronic inflammatory demyelinating polyneuropathy (CIDP)<- UpToDate mentions a polyradiculoneuropathy.
  • Bowel and bladder dysfunction at onset makes GBS unlikely.

    • B cell numbers are normal but there are low levels of isotype switched B memory cells and plasma cells.

Complications:

  • Lymphoma

Chronic giadia infections with malabsorption can occur.

Agammaglobulinaemia

#2021BSQ-JUL Q24
Also called hypogammaglobulinaemia.

Mutation of the BTK gene impairs B cell maturation -> causes low or absent mature B cells -> severe hypogammaglobulinaemia.
Commonest inheritance: #x-linked-recessive with mothers being carriers. #autosomal-Recessive inheritance is seen in 50%.

Manifestation: recurrent sinopulmonary infections.(From 6 to 12 months of age, otitis media, sinusitis, bronchitis, and pneumonia).
Common pathogens: Encapsulated pyogenic bacteria Haemophillus influenzae and strep pneumoniae.

Treatment: repeaded IVIG transfusions or stem cell transplant.
