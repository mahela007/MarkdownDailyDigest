[!INFO] Type 1 Vs. Type 2
Type 1: "more cysts, more frequent, more chromosomes"

Mutations in PKD1 gene (on chromosome 16) are responsible for 85% of cases. Rest are by PKD2 gene (on chromosome 4).

[!INFO] Autosomal recessive PCKD
Due to #autosomal-Recessive mutations carried on chromosome 6. Much rarer than ADPKD.

PKD genes encode the polycystin proteins. Mutations of these cause decreased intracellular calcium in the principal cells of the collecting duct -> dysoriented cell division -> cyst formation.

Polycystin 1 (in PKD1) is a regulator of tubular epithelial cell adhesion and differentiation. Polycystin 2 (in PKD2) is an ion channel. These are required for function of cilia of the tubular epithelium which are in turn required for flow sensing. Impaired flow sensing causes a defect in proliferation and differentiation -> cysts.

Cyst formation occurs only in 5-10% of nephrons but that's enough to cause renomegaly.

Risk factors for progressive renal disease:

  • Male gender
  • proteinuria - although it's not a major feature of PCKD.
  • High urinary sodium excretion
  • hypertension - patients with larger kidneys tend to have hypertension.
    PKD1 is more severe than PKD2

Since the cysts grow at the same rate in both forms, it is likely that the difference is due to a higher number of cysts in type 1, rather than a higher growth rate. Source
