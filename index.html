Presentations of MS

  • Usually has a area of 4cm2.
  • Narrowing of upto 2 cm2 -> patients are asymptomatic.
  • Severe symptoms when area :down to 1cm2.
  • Symptoms: dyspnoea - due to pulmonary oedema.
  • Blood tinged cough - rupture of pulmonary arteries / or haemoptysis.
  • Features of secondary right heart failure.
  • Enlarged atrium -> AF, left atrial thrombi, emboli,
  • Commonest cause is rheumatic carditis.
  • Signs: Mitral facies (due to formation of arteriovenous anastomoses).
  • Active filling plays a bigger part in ventricular filling -> presystolic accentuation of the murmur.
  • Loud P2 after development of pulmonary hypertension.
  • Can have P mitrale with bifid P wave.
  • MS murmur = best heart in expiration, at apex.

Source - UpToDate

  • FSGS - Focal areas of mesangial collapse and sclerosis.
  • Causes: Idiopathic, HIV infection, previous glomerular disease, massive obesity.
  • In FSGS, disctintion between primary and secondary FSGS is important as treatment differs
  • Clinical picture is ==more towards nephritic== in secondary FSGS.
  • FSGS recurs in transplanted kidneys.
  • Corresponding with the focal nature of the sclerosis, most of the affected glomeruli are at the level of the corticomedullary junction; so superficial biopsies can miss the affected glomeruli .
  • Haematuria is commoner in FSGS than in MCD.
  • MCD shows selective protein loss -> i.e mainly albumin and not higher molecular weight proteins like immunoglobulins. FSGS shows non-selective proteinuria.
  • FSGS shows poor response to corticosteroids.
  • Higher percentage of FSGS patients go on to develop CKD (50%). (adults have worse prognosis)
  • MCD and FSGS both show minimal / no immune deposits.
  • FSGS : FSGS changes are visible on light microscopy. MCD: few changes visible on light microscopy.

Low toxicity; Non sedative.

  • Stabilization of voltage gated Na channels.
  • Increases GABA levels. (GABA is made by the TCA cycle and valproate inhibits enzymes which degrade it).

This binding extends the inactivated phase and inhibits the generation of rapid action potentials when the cell is experiencing incoming depolarizing trains - i.e stabilizes voltage gated sodium channels
