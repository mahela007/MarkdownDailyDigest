  • Produced by parafollicular C cells of the thyroid;
  • Despite medullary thyroid CA patients having very high calcitonin levels, their calcium levels are not abnormal.
  • Effect: It inhibits osteoclastic bone resorption and increases urinary calcium excretion.
  • Calcitonin is elevated in Zollinger-Ellison syndrome and in Pernicious anaemia.
  • Calcitonin half life is only 10 minutes.
  • Not an important hormone in normal physiology.
  • Unlike Vitamin D and PTH, calcitonin is not involved in phosphate homeostasis.
  • May be important in pregnancy because it protects the mother's bone against reabsorption when producing milk and providing calcium for foetal bones.

Indications for therapeutic used:

  • postmenopausal osteoporosis
  • Paget's disease of bone
  • Hypercalcemia

[!INFO] Osteoporotic fractures are extremely common.
In 2 women and 1 in 5 men over 50 years will have one at some point.

[!INFO] Relationship between serum calcium and osteoporosis
Many factors increase bone resorption. See bone metabolism note.
With increasing age, there is decreasing ability of the kidneys to activate Vitamin D.
This tends to reduce serum calcium leading compensatory hyperparathyroidism.
The hyperparathyroidism causes bone resorption.
Despite normal calcium levels in osteoporotic patients, because of the mechanism above, dietary calcium supplementation can offset the body's need to resorb bone.

Intestinal calcium absorption and the ability to adapt to low calcium diets are impaired in many postmenopausal women and elderly persons owing to a suspected functional or absolute decrease in the ability of the kidney to produce 1,25(OH)2D3. The bones then become more and more a source of calcium to maintain critical extracellular fluid calcium levels. Available evidence suggests that the impairments of intestinal calcium absorption observed during the menopause and aging can be overcome only by inordinately large calcium intakes (1500 to 2500 mg/day). Since this amount is difficult to derive from the diet, can cause constipation, and may not prevent trabecular bone loss, it should not be used as a substitute for sex hormone replacement. Women taking estrogen replacement should be provided the RDA for calcium of 800 mg/day at a minimum. Those who cannot or will not take estrogen should be asked to ingest at least 1000 to 1500 mg/day of calcium to delay cortical bone loss and prevent secondary hyperparathyroidism. Source

Effects of Noradrenaline on kidneys

  • Increased renin release (by acting on renal beta 1 receptors)
  • Increased sodium reabsorption (direct action on tubules)
  • Sympathetic stimulation of the kidneys by noradrenaline mediated nerves reduces renal blood flow. This is important in the effector response of the barroreceptor reflex.
    • So during hypotension, exercise and rising from supine position renal blood flow is reduced.
