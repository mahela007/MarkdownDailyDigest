The classic mid-diastolic low-pitched rumbling murmur of mitral stenosis with pre-systolic accentuation is best audible at the apex, in the left lateral position with the bell of the stethoscope. Pre-systolic accentuation usually means the presence of atrial contraction and hence sinus rhythm, but it may persist in AF following short diastoles [6]. The duration of the murmur is directly proportional to the severity of mitral stenosis, but the intensity of the murmur is not. The murmur can be heard provided the atrioventricular gradient remains above 3 mmHg [3]. Because diastolic mean gradient is directly related to heart rate, the manoeuvres that increase heart rate can help to detect an otherwise faint murmur . Source

Basis of macular sparing in occpital lobe infarction:

The tip of the occipital lobe which receives macular retinal signals has dual blood supply from the PCA and MCA. Therefore, occlusion of either one of the arteries will result in macular sparing. Source
