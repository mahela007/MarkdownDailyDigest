Thiamine and deficiency

Source
Water soluble.
Not stored in significant amounts-> constant intake required. (Present in whole grain, brown rise, nuts, beans, peas, and pork + poultry. )
Causes of low Thiamine: Dietary deficiency and impaired absorption can cause deficiency. (Alcohol dependency, AIDS, post-bariatric surgery, pregnancy, lactation, haemodyalisys, renal failure, hyperthyroidism are all causes).
The active form is thiamine triphosphate which is an essential co-enzyme in glycolysis, Kreb's cycle, TCA cycle and overall metabolism of carbohydrates, lipids and amino acids.

[!TIP]
So, it makes sense that deficiency affects metabolically active tissues like heart and nerves.

Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome

Consists of acute/subacute confusional state and often reversible findings of Wernicke encephalopathy (a type of delirium) and by persistent and irreversible findings of Korsakoff dementia. (See Clinical and Workup.)
Caused by prolonged thiamine deficiency.
The name describes to disease patterns caused by thiamine deficiency.
Wernicke encephalopathy

  • Mnemonic : Altered mental state + eye signs + ataxia
  • acute condition; Reversible; requires emergency treatment to prevent death.
  • Triad of ophthalmoplegia, ataxia, confusion, + nystagmus + lower limb polyneuropathy.
  • The hallmark sign of Wernicke encephalopathy is ocular abnormalities especially nystagmus
  • Occurs in chronic alcoholics and after bariatric surgery.
  • Other causes: severe malnutrition, hyperemesis gravidarum, prolonged parenteral nutrition, malignancies, immunodeficiency syndromes, liver disease, hyperthyroidism, and severe anorexia nervosa

Korsakoff syndrome:

  • chronic features, irreversible.
  • Only notable for loss of short term memory and confabulations.
  • Confabulations are a manifestation of anterograde amnenia.
  • Impaired executive function - planning, problem solving, judgement.

Anterograde amnesia (AA) - can't learn new thing
Retrograde Amnesia (RA) - Forgets old information

Beri beri

Dry beriberi is the development of a symmetrical peripheral neuropathy characterized by both sensory and motor impairments, mostly of the distal extremities.(peripheral neuropathy, muscle weakness, brisk tendon reflexes, seizures).

Wet beriberi includes signs of cardiac involvement with cardiomegaly, cardiomyopathy, High output heart failure, peripheral edema, and tachycardia, in addition to neuropathy

DKA Vs. HHS (hyperosmolar hyperglycaemic state)

HHS is also known as hyperosmolar hyperglycaemic nonketotic state (HHNK)
HHS usually occurs with prexisting disease.

  • Serum glucose frequently exceeds 1000 in HHS but is usually in the 350-450 range.
  • Two proposed reasons for observing lower glucose levels in DKA
    • Patients with DKA present earlier with symptoms. Symptoms of hyperosmolarity tend to present later. (so there's more time for hyperglycemia to develop)
    • Patients with DKA are younger and usually present early in the development of Diabetes. Young patients, and those with early diabetes have elevated GFR. Therefore, DKA patients usually have high GFR which causes a lot of glucose to be lost via the urine -> this limits serum glucose concentration.

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD) is a heterogeneous lung condition characterized by chronic respiratory symptoms (dyspnea, cough, sputum production and/or exacerbations) due to abnormalities of the airways (bronchitis, bronchiolitis) and/or alveoli (emphysema) that cause persistent, often progressive, airflow obstruction.

Causes of lactic acidosis

Overview of lactate metabolism:
Glucose is anaerobically converted to pyruvate by glycloysis (also called the Embden-Myerhof-Parnas pathway).
Pyruvate can enter the Kreb's cycle within mitochondria Or it can be converted to lactate by the enzyme lactate dehydrogenase.
Therefore, lactate and pyruvate exist in an equilibrium.

Some tissues (heart, liver and kidneys) can use lactate as an energy source by converting it back to pyruvate for use in aerobic metabolism.
Alternatively, the liver and kidneys can convert lactate to glucose via gluconeogensis.
The cycle of some organs converting glucose to lactate and other organs converting lactate to glucose is known as the Cori cycle.
