Paroxysmal cold haemoglobinuria

  • Also called Donath-Landsteiner anaemia.
  • Caused by IgG. (usually IgG causes warm AIHA)
  • It is a biphasic antibody which binds in the cold (peripheries) and causes lysis when rewarmed.
  • Mainly occurs in children but can occur in adults with tertiary syphillis or haematologic malignancies.

Myelodysplastic syndromes

  • Commonly found in aging population
  • All are characterized by one or more peripheral blood cytopenias.
  • Bone marrow is usually hypercellular, but rarely hypocellular.
  • High risk of progression to [[Haematologic malignancies-Summary#Acute myeloid leukemia#AML]]
  • Bone marrow cells display aberrant morphology and maturation (dysmyelopoiesis), resulting in ineffective blood cell production.
  • usually cause macrocytic anaemias.

[!INFO] Intermediate syndrome
- Ten to 40 percent of patients poisoned with organophosphorus develop a distinct neurologic disorder 24 to 96 hours after exposure and resolution of cholinergic excess.
- Mainly Proximal Muscle weakness: characteristic neurological findings including neck flexion weakness, decreased deep tendon reflexes, cranial nerve abnormalities, proximal muscle weakness, and respiratory insufficiency
- Thought to result from dysfunction of the nicotinic receptors due to prolonged activation.
- Complete resolution occurs in 2-3 weeks if the patient receives ventilatory support
