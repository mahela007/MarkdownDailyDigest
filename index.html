Filaria

#2023BSQ-NOV/Q

  • immune response to parasites causes hyperplasia of lymphatic vessels.
  • Adult worms also cause physical obstruction of lymph vessels.
  • Both cause lymphatic obstruction.

Clinical features

  • Acute presentations (lasts 4 - 7 days)
    • Adenolyphangitis -> Acute fever and painful lymphadenopathy.
    • Lymphangitis - inflammtion along lymphatic drainage.
    • Filarial fever:
      • Only fever
    • Dermatolymphangioadenitis
      • Oedematous inflammatory plaques;
      • Superadded bacterial infection of the skin
    • Tropical pulmonary eosinophilia
      • Microfilaria get stuck in pulmonary capillaries
      • no filaria visible in the blood. (also called OCCULT FILARIASIS
      • Presents as dry cough with diurnal variation
        Chronic
  • Lymphoedema

Diagnosis:

  • peak blood concentrations of parasite:
    • 10PM to 2AM.
  • Serology:
    • Antigen: adult worms release antigens when they die.
    • Antibodies - not usually used.
  • Eosinophilia with elevated IgE.
  • microscopic haematuria and proteinuria.
  • DEC provocation test:
    • Dose of DEC will promote migration of lung capillary microfilaria to migrate into peripheral blood.

Treatment:

  • TPE - diethyl carbamazepine for 21 days.
  • Management:

Post partum thyroiditis.

  • characteristic sequence of hyperthyroidism, which usually begins one to four months after delivery and lasts two to eight weeks, followed by hypothyroidism, which lasts from approximately two weeks to six months, and then recovery
  • Vector is anopheles mosquito (which is present in Sri Lanka)
  • Knowlesi causes malaria in monkeys; it can infect humans as an accidental host. But it's mainly a zoonosis. #2023BSQ-NOV/Q54. No human to human transmission reported.
  • Although falciparum causes most deaths, vivax has the larger geographical range.

The life cycles of P. vivax and P. ovale include a dormant hepatic stage called the hypnozoite. (hypnozoite can last upto 5 years)
