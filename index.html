[!INFO] The HOCM murmur
#2020SBR-JUL/Q11
The murmur is a high-pitched, crescendo-decrescendo, mid-systolic murmur usually heard best at the left lower sternal border; it typically ends before the second heart sound. It does not radiate to the carotids like that of aortic stenosis (AS), but may radiate to the base and apex of the heart.

[!INFO] "The HOCM murmur is louder when the ventricular volume is low, as the outflow tract is narrower".
The murmur and the gradient across the LVOT will decrease with

  • an increase in preload (Squatting) or
  • an increase in afterload (handgrip). Source

The gradient and the murmur will increase with

  • a decrease in preload (Valsalva maneuver, diuretics, standing). Certain maneuvers can affect murmurs auscultated in HOCM.

Goal of treatment is to prevent recurrences and promote healing of existing lesions.

Crohn's disease

Induction of remission

  • steroids - oral or IV
    • In midly active Crohn's disease with low risk, oral budesonide is used because is has low systemic absorption.
    • IV steroids are used for severe disease. Source
  • Second line agent - anti TNF-alpha antibodies -> infliximab, adalimumab; best used in combination with azathioprine.
    • (can be used as steroid sparing agents)
    • They induce healing of lesions
    • Intermittent dosing leads to formation of inactivating antibodies -> regular dosing is required.
  • Azathioprine, mercaptopurine and MTX can also be used.
  • Exclusive enteral nutrition helps to induce remission.
  • aminosalicilates are of NO benefit.

Maintenance

Except for steroids, the others can be used for maintenance.

Ulcerative colitis

Induction of remission

  • Local 5 amino salicilates are the mainstay of treatment. (Mesalazine , Olsalazine, Balsazide, Sulfasalazine)
  • They act topically.
  • Delivered via enema (for left sided colitis) or suppository (proctitis) or oral for extensive disease.
  • Mechanism of action uknown.
  • Second line options for resistant disease: anti TNF-alpha inhibitors (infliximab, adalimumab) or anti integrin (vedolizumab), or tofacitinib.
  • Severe colitis - IV hydrocortisone with enoxaparin to prevent thromboembolism.
