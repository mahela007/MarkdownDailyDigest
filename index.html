  • 'Antiphopholipid' antibodies is a misnomer because they are actually antibodies against certain proteins.
  • "Antiphospholipid antibodies" is a collective term for several of antibodies including anti cardiolipin antibodies and lupus anticoagulant.
  • The most important of these proteins is anti-beta2 glycprotein I. (anti Beta2-GPI)
  • This group also includes anti-cardiolipin antibodies.
    aPL is also directed against a variety of other antigenic targets such as prothrombin (PT), phosphatidylserine/prothrombin (PS/PT) complexes, vimentin/cardiolipin complexes, annexin A2, lupus anticoagulant and annexin A5
    Lupus anticoagulants: (a type of antiphopholipid antibody) actually refers to an anticoagulant effect of several types of antibodies.
    Management: Anticoagulation during pregnancy.

Unfractionated heparin is preferred over LMW heparin in patients with severely reduced kidney function because LMW heparin clearance is exclusively by the kidney, while elimination of unfractionated heparin is by the kidney AND liver.

[!INFO] Pathophysiology details: Skip this.
ADAMTS degrades the vWF multimers and prevents thrombus formation particularly in high sheer stress areas like small arterioles and capillaries. Shear stress leads to a conformational change in the large VWF multimers that exposes the ADAMTS13 cleavage site

  • The major cause of severe ADAMTS13 deficiency is an autoantibody. Inherited gene mutations account for a minority.

  • Patients with ADAMTS13 deficiency usually get TTP when they encounter a TRIGGER such as infection or pregnancy.

  • This is the dangerous one.

  • 50% of deaths occurs in first 24 hours!

[!INFO] Plasmapheresis Vs. Plasma exchange
Plasmapheresis is the process of separating plasma from blood. Plasma exchange is the process of substituting the separated plasma with another fluid (albumin or donor plasma)
