[!TIP] Overall picture
There is reversible airway obstruction in asthma.
It occurs in twophases:

  1. Early phase - Airway narrowing due to predominantly ASM mediated bronchoconstriction by mast cell released histamine + other mediators
  2. Late phase - More aiway narrowing due to further bronchoconstriction + local inflammation and oedema -> stimulated by inflammatory cells migrating to the mucosa during the late phase

variable narrowing of airway lumens causing variable reduction in airflow is pathognomonic of asthma.

Mechanisms of narrowing :

  1. Contraction of airway smooth muscle. <-mast cell and eosinophil mediators, such as leukotrienes C4, D4, and E4 and histamine, are potent bronchoconstrictors
  2. Thickening of the airway wall due to cells (inflammatory cell migration) or oedema
  3. plugging with mucus / debris
  4. airway remodelling

Bronchial hyper-responsiveness:
Another definingb feature of asthma = exagerrated reversible airway obstruction due to ASM contraction to physical, chemical or environmental stimuli. What causes airway smooth muscle to become hyper responsive to these stimuli is not known. Airway remodelling and altered function and mass of ASM could play a part.

Mast cell activation due to various other effectors is main factor in pathogenesis of asthma.
Mast cell activation by allergen serves as an example for what happens in asthma
4. But smooth muscle contraction isn't the only mechanism here since beta agonists don't completely reverse late phase bronchoconstriction.
1. In late phase, inflammatory cell migration, mucous plugging, oedema all contibute to further narrowing.

Innate immune system also plays a part in the pathogenesis of asthma. -> cells of the innate immune system are also found in excess in asthmatic airways.

Exhaled nitric oxide

Nitric oxide acts in the lungs to dilate both airways and blood vessels.
In asthma, epithelial cells produce NO probably to counteract the narrowing caused by other mediators.
Exhaled NO can be used as a marker of the degree of eosinophillic aiway inflammation. ->
i.e Exhaled NO increases in asthma.
