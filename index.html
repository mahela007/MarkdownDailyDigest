Essential / primary hypertension

  • 90% of hypertensives have Primary hypertension - i.e no identifiable cause.
    • BP is influenced by many genes. Each one has a tiny effect on it's own.
    • Specially in this group, diet and lifetyle have definite influences on blood pressure

There are various mechanisms described for the development of hypertension which includes 

  • increased salt absorption resulting in volume expansion,
  • an impaired response of the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS),
  • increased activation of the sympathetic nervous system.
  • These changes lead to the development of increased total peripheral resistance and increased afterload which in turn leads to the development of hypertension.

Kidney transplants from hypertensive patients induce hypertension in the recipient and transplants from normotensive donors reduce BP in hypertensive patients - This shows the close relationship between kidneys and blood pressure regulation. - Rang and Dale's Pharmacology.

Secondary hypertension

  • 10% of patients have Secondary hypertension - (higher proportion in those < 30 years old)
    • Commonest causes of secondary hypertension are
      1. Primary Hyperaldoesteronism - increased circulatory volume
      2. Obstrucive sleep apnoea
      3. Obesity
      • Obesity and OSA cause increased sympathetic tone. Leptin secreted by adipocytes has a sympathetic stimulatory activity.
    • Other causes of secondary hypertension
      • Pregnancy - usually BP drops in #pregnancy (Volume↑, but tone ↓↓ = lower BP) but some patients have PIH and pre-eclampsia.
      • Renal - renal artery stenosis, CKD
      • Vascular - Coarcation of aorta
      • Neural - autonomic dysfunction
      • Endocrine - ↑ Aldosterone, ↓ Cortisol, Phaechromocytoma, Hypo And Hyper thyroidism, Acromegaly

Definition of hypertension

AHA guidelines . SBP > 130 , DBP > 80
European : SBP > 140, DBP > 90.
Based on average of two or more readings taken at two or more visits.
BP cuff should cover 80% of the upper arm.
