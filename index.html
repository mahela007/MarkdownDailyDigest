[!INFO] OA isn't just wear and tear - Summary
It starts with cartilage chrondomalacia - breakdown of the cartilage.
Underlying bone becomes exposed -> leads to eburnation.
There is also subchontral thickening - Sclerosis.
Pieces of bone break off and float around -> joint mice
Subchondral bone is sclerosed.
Advanced cases show osteophyte formation, subchondral bone cysts, and subchondral sclerosis.
Disease process is mediated by inflammatory mediators but is mainly a degenerative process, not an inflammatory process.

  • OA is considered a disease of the joint, as a whole organ.
  • Destruction of articular cartilage is a central feature.
  • However, all other joint tissues are affected. (ligaments, tendon, synovium, menisci, subchondral bone, capsule).
  • Pro inflammatory mediators secreted by joint cells are involved in the pathogenesis but classical inflammation is NOT PRESENT and the disease process is primarily degenerative, not inflammatory - Robbins.
  • Mediators : (probably IL-1, IL-6 and TNF) which also stimulate the action of proteases (matrix metalloproteinases MMP) that mediate extracellular matrix destruction.
  • Synovial joint fluid only has mild leukocytosis. (but in contrast, is high in RA)
  • Sub chondral bone becomes sclerotic
    • (after exposure from destruction of overlying cartilage) - there is also hypercellularity of the subchondral bone;
    • (Mnemonic: like caramel pudding instead of gelatin)
  • Pieces of bone and cartilage are disloged into the sinovial fluid - synovial 'mice'.

[!TIP] Mnemonic: "Hand is Dipped" in OA.

  • Over years, inflammation settles leaving behind bony nodules on the posterolateral aspect of interphalangeal joints.
  • DIP more frequently involved than PIP.
    Also, in OA joints don't appear inflamed on examination; but in RA, joints are warm, soft and boggy.

Pharmacologic management of osteoarthritis

Source

  • Knee OA : topical NSAIDS; NSAIDs may be useful for other joints as well.
  • NSAID with PPI
  • Paracetamol and weak opioids should not to be used routinely.
    • Can be used infrequently for short term.
    • If other treatments are contraindicated.
  • Glucosamine - has no benefit.
  • Strong opioids - risks outweigh benefits.
  • Intra-articular hyaluronan has no benefit.
  • Intra-articular corticosteroids can be used when all above have failed.

NSAID comparison

Source - Katzung

  • All including aspirin, are equally efficacious.
    • Tolmemetin is best for gout.
  • Ketorolac is known for renal and GI side effects.
  • Indomethacin and tolmetin may be the most toxic.
  • Least toxic: ibuprofen, aspirin.
  • Diclofenac: more liver function test abnormalities than other NSAIDs.
  • Ibuprofen antagonizes the antiplatelet effect of aspirin.
  • COX-2 selectives has lower GI bleeding risk but higher cardiovascular risk.
