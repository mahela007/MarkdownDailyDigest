Megaloblasts present in bone marrow AND Oval macrocytes on the blood smear.

Megaloblastic states result from defective DNA synthesis. However, RNA synthesis continues, resulting in a large cell with a large nucleus.
Therefore, characteristically, megaloblasts in the bone marrow have immature nuclei compared to the degree of cytoplasmic maturation.

Treatment of B12 and folate deficiency

If large doses of folate are given in B12 deficiency, neuropathy can be worsened!.
B9 (folate) should not be given alone unless B12 deficiency is excluded.
B12 is given IM and B9 is given orally.

Reticulocytes and assessment of erythropoiesis

Reticulocytes are 'immature RBCs'. Their cytoplasm still contains RNA.
In intact erythropoiesis, reticulocyte count increases in proportion to the anaemia.
Erythropoiesis is stimulated by EPo which is produced by the peritubular, interstitial cells of the kidney in response to reduced oxygen delivery.
Timescale: after acute haemorrhage, EPO increases by 6 hours, retic count begins to increase in 2-3 days and peaks by 6-10 days.
