Increases the density of beta receptors in skeletal muscle (and adipose tissue) Source |

  • Common cause of hyperthyroidism

Pathology:

  • Histology: Diffuse, hyperplasia and hypertrophy;
  • Follicular cells become tall and columnar with crowding and formation of papillae with no neovascularization.
    • T cell infiltrates are present within thyroid.

Clinical feature:

  • Orbitopathy and pretibial myxoedema are not related to thyroxine levels. Pretibial myxoedema is caused by deposition of glycosaminoglycans (also known as mucopolysaccharides) and T cell infiltration.

The more recent term for this phemomenon is "Thyroid associated orbitopathy" - reflecting that it's a pathology of the orbital tissue and not of the eye as 'ophthalmopathy' would indicate.

However the terms are still used interchangeably.
A complex series of events causes orbital fibroblasts to produced glycosaminoglycans which accumulate (also increase osmolarity -> orbital oedema) which cause proptosis.

Cavernous sinus thrombosis

  • Facial vein, sphenoid vein drains into cavernous sinus. (blood from face, nose tonsils and orbits)
  • No valves in the venous network - infections can easily spread to cavernous sinus.
  • Sources say that the internal carotid is almost nerver affected in CST despite being located in the CS.
  • Sphenoid and ethmoid sinusis lead to CST more commonly than frontal sinus thrombosis.
  • Commonest organism: staph aureus, or strep pneumoniae.
  • headache, fever, peri orbital oedema, cranial nerve signs.
  • Venous obstruction: Chemosis (ophthalmic vein obstruction), PO oedema
    • eye swelling is initially unilateral -> but without treatment will spread to the other eye in 24-48 hours. This is pathogmonomic. (because of the intercavernous connection)
  • Nerves affected: CN 3 and CN 4 and CN 6.
  • Exophthalmos
  • Hyperaesthesis or hypoesthesia in V1 and V2 distributin.
  • Nuchal rigidity, kernig's, Brudzinski sign,
  • Chronic sinusis and DM impose slightly higher risk of CST.
  • Treatment: Cover with AB with +ve and -ve cover
    • IV ABx recommended for 3 to 4 weeks.
    • Early and aggressive ABx administration.
