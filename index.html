[!TIP] mnemonic: The overall metabolic effect is "mobilize and burn".

System effect
CVS Increase cardiac ouput ( increased heart rate, contractility, stroke volume)
RS Maintains respiratory drive
GI increases motility
Blood Incr. 23-DPG - increased oxygen unloading
Muscles Increase speed of muscle contraction
Carbs Increase gluconeogenesis, glycolysis, intestinal glucose absorption, cellular glucose reabsorption, but 'no effect on glucose level' (probably because it increases insulin secretion )
lipid Lipolysis, increased serum free fatty acids (which can be oxidised), decreased serum cholesterol,
Lipid cont. PL and TGL + increased cholesterol turnover (patients lose weight), increased liver LDL receptors (see below)
Proteins Anabolism Source but at high doses, catabolism.
Sympathetic NS Increases expression of beta adrenergic receptors and in heart decreases alpha receptors. See Perissive effect below:
At cellular level Thyroid hormones No. of mitochondria, increase activity of Na/K ATPase -> thermogenesis, increaes Na and K condutivity
Sexual function hypothyroidism in women -> menorrhagia or oligomenorrhea, decreased libido in men and women.

More details on the above:

  • Thyroid hormones have a permissive effect on catecholamines.
  • In the heart, this increases heart rate, stroke volume.
    • It increases rate of depolarization, repolarization and decreases duration of action potentials. Probably increases dromotropy as well. Effects are probably mediated by increased expression of Na/K ATPase and increased ion permeability. Source <- super detailed, don't read. ;)
  • In hyperthyroidism, there is widened pulse pressure. (contributed to be decreased peripheral resistance). Source
  • To emphasize the effect on lipid metabolism, hypothyroidism causes hypercholesterolemia.
  • Thyroid hormone (and hyperthyroidism) increases hepatic LDL receptors which promotes biliary excretion of cholesterol (leading to lower cholesterol levels)
