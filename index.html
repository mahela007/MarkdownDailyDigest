Other hormones regulating blood glucose level

#2021BSQ-NOV Q07

  • Catecholamines increase hepatic glucose output;
  • Epinephrine (adrenaline) decreases peripheral uptake of glucose.
  • Thyroxine: hyperthyroidism exacerbates diabetes. Thyroxine increases gut absorption of glucose and potentiate catecholamine action (which is as above)
  • Glucocorticoids: Have a permissive effect on the actions of glucagon and attenuating action on insulin.
    • They are necessary for glucagon mediated gluconeogenesis during fasting.
    • Hypoglycaemic effect of insulin is much stronger (even too strong!) in the absence of glucocorticoids.
    • Both effects above cause hypoglycaemia seen in Addison's disease.
    • The diabetogenic effects are due to increase in protein catabolism, increased gluconeogenesis and decreased peripheral utilization of blucose.
  • Exercise: not a hormone BUT
    • Exercise causes upregulation of GLUT-4 transported in muscle and this causes insulin independent increase in glucose uptake.

Glucose transporters

[!TIP] Mnemonic
GLUT-2 and GLUT-4 : even numbered ones are involved in insulin mediated glucose regulation.
1 and 3 are basal, non insulin dependent transporters.

Transporter Site
SGLT-1 small intestine and renal tubules
SGLT-2 renal tubules
GLUT 1,3 basal glucose uptake 'everywhere'
GLUT 2 beta cell glucose sensor
GLUT 4 insulin mediated glucose uptake in Sk and cardiac muscle, adipocytes
GLUT 5 Fructose transporter

Theory and equations

Source

[!INFO] Definition
Difference in oxygen concentration between the alveoli and the arterial system.
$$
A-a\space gradient = PAO_2 - PaO_2
$$
A - alveolar, a - arterial.

But how do we know what the PAO2 is? Well it's estimated using the alveolar gas equation.

[!INFO] Alveolar gas equation
$$
PAO_2 = (P_atm - P_H2O) FiO_2 - PaCO_2/RQ
$$
RQ = respiratory quotient. Varies depending on which substrate is being used for aerobic respiration (i.e carbs vs. fats etc).
RQ = amount of CO2 produced / amount of oxygen consumed.

The alveolar gas equations shows us the main parameters which affect alveolar PO2. (PAO2)

The main variables quantities in the alveolar gas equation are

  1. Atmospheric pressure
  2. FiO2
  3. Levels of CO2.

[!INFO]
Theoretically, there the A-a gradient should be zero as oxygen should equilibriate between alveoli and the capillaries.
But in reality, an A-a gradient arises because of non uniformity of V/Q ratios throughout the lung.
