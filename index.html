Some forms of familial distal [[2021-SBR-November#Renal tubular acidosis|renal tubular acidosis]] arises from band-3 malfunction.

  • In these situations, the 'apparent' low intravascular volume acts to decrease renal perfusion pressure, decrease GFR, and increase PCT Na resorption. All these reduce the delivery of Na to the DCT. These processes impair aldosterone escape and blunt the actions of atrial natriuretic peptide. (ANP) leading to impaired aldosterone escape and oedema.
  • The glucose and amino acids in food have largely been absorbed by the time food makes it to the ileum. However, a significant amount of long chain fatty acids are absorbed in the ileum.
  • Bile salts are reabsorbed in the ileum.
    • Bile salt reabsorption is required for absorption of fats and lipids because the liver cannot synthesize new bile salts fast enough to replace losses. Ileal reabsorption is ‚≠êactive. Therefore, with ileal resection, there is defective fat absorption.
    • When unabsorbed bile salts enter the colon, they promote chloride secretion and cause diarrhoea.
  • Most vitamins are absorbed in the proximal small intestine but absorption of vitamin B12, complexed to intrinsic factor, occurs in the small intestine.
  • The ileum is 'relatively sterile' compared to the colon and colonic reflux is prevented by the ileocoecal valve.
  • Hypocalcemia is a possible complication of intestinal resection (Ganong's - clinical box 24-6)
    • The duodenum (and possibly upper jejunum) actively absorb calcium but most (70-80%) of the calcium is absorded in the ileum.
    • The colon may also pay a part in calcium absorption. Source
