Single nodule, multiple nodules or Grave's.

Overview of evaluation of hyperthyroidism

  • Grave's disease is diagnosed based on classical clinical findings and history; if these are absent, other diagnoses are checked for.
  • Second line investigation is radioiodine uptake.

  • Disease follows a relapsing remitting course. Majority have recurrences.
  • Orbitopathy and pretibial myxoedema are not related to thyroxine levels. Orbitopathy does not correlate with disease activity.
  • Dermopathy
    • Pretibial myxoedema is caused by deposition of glycosaminoglycans (also known as mucopolysaccharides) and T cell infiltration.
  • Acropachy - a form of clubbing seen in Grave's disease; strongly associated with dermopathy.

4 Features of Grave's ophthalmopathy: (Harrison's)

  1. Lid retraction
  2. Periorbital oedema
  3. Conjunctival injection
  4. Proptosis
  5. and diplopia -> can occur late due to fibrosis of extraocular muscles.

As above, lid retraction is more suggestive of Grave's ophthalmopathy.
"Lid retraction (which causes a staring appearance) can occur in any form of thyrotoxicosis".
