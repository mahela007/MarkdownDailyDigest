Drug development

[!INFO] Key points:
Preclinical testing:
to find out if the drug is safe to test in humans.

  1. To determine drug side effects
  2. To determine maximum tolerable doses
  3. Phamacokinetic testing to determine ADME chara (absorption, distribution, metabolism and elimination)

Clinical testing:
Phase 1 = 20-80 healthy volunteers; study safety, and identify side effects.
Phase 2 = 100-300 patients to assess efficacy and dosage.
Phase 3 = 1000-3000 to confirm effectiveness, monitor side effects and compare with other treatments.
Phase 4 = post marketing surveillance

Argyll-robertson (argyll robertson) pupil is a highly specific manifestation of late neurosyphillis. Usually affects bilateral pupils. The pupil constricts during accommodation but does not constrict in response to light.
Mechanism isn't known.

[!INFO]

Probably due to dorsal midbrain lesion that interrupts the pupillary light reflex pathway but spares the more ventral pupillary near reflex pathway.Source
This is called light-near dissociation.

Holes-Adie syndrome

#2020SBR-JUL/Q22
AKA Adie syndrome
Source

  1. unilateral or bilateral tonically dilated pupils with
  2. near - light dissociation and
  3. tendon areflexia.

It's 'relatively common' and has female predominance.
When associated with hypohydrosis, it is called Ross syndrome.
Mostly idiopathic but can also be caused by

lesions involving the orbit that affect the ciliary ganglion including infections such as syphilis, varicella, human parvovirus-B19, human immunodeficiency virus, and Lyme disease,

The pathophysiology is convoluted.

- Horizontal nystagmus is typical. 
- Vertical nystagmus can occur.
