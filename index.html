The place of frusemide in AKI:

Source

[!INFO] Generally, a positive frusemide response indicates a better prognosis.

Because frusemide is highly protein bound, it isn't filtered.
For it to act at the TAL, Frusemide is actively secreted into the tubule and travels along with the urine to reach the TAL where it acts;
therefore, some GFR is needed for frusemide to act; conversely a 'frusemide response' means that some GFR is still there.

Being bound to proteins facilitates the secretion of frusemide by the PCT.
Therefore, low protein states and drugs displacing frusemide from protein (warfarin, phenytoin) will ameliorate it's diuretic effect.
Giving albumin in hypoalbuminaemic states will restore the diuretic effect of Frusemide.

Giving very high doses of frusemide (>1000mg) in AKI can cause it to accumulate (because it's renally eliminated) and this can cause ototoxicity.

Frusemide doesn't seem to have a direct nephrotoxic effect but the hypovolemia they cause by venodilationa and diuresis can induce RAAS activation and reduce renal perfusion.
Source

Effects of corticosteroids

The diverse effects of corticosteroids basically include
Source

  1. Alteration of lipid, carbohydrate and protein metabolism
  2. Modulation of electrolyte and water balance.
  3. Complex interactions with other hormones
    1. Permissive action on glucagon and catecholamines

Corticosteroid molecules are either gluco or mineralocorticoids.

  1. Glucocorticoids: predominantly act on intermediary metabolism, inflammation, immunity, wound healing, myocardial, and muscle integrity
  2. Mineralocorticoids: salt and water balance.
  • Metabolic action: Glucocorticoids promote gluconeogensis by protein catabolism and simultaneous glycogenesis.
