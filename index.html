Chronic kidney disease management

Stages of kidney disease

[!INFO] Definition of CKD
Presence of

  1. kidney damage (seen as urinary albumin excretion > 30 mg / day)
  2. (more than 300 mg/ day is called macroalbuminuria)
  3. decreased kidney function (seen as eGFR < 60 )
    For 3 or more months.

Complications of CKD

  1. Complications directly related to loss of kidney function:
    1. Volume overload, hyperkalemia, metabolic acidosis, hyperphosphatemia
  2. Systemic complications:
    1. anorexia, nausea, fatigue, malnutrition
    2. hyperlipidaemia
    3. hypertension
    4. anaemia
    5. bone disease

There is associated increased risk of

  1. Cardiovascular disease
  2. progression to end stage kidney disease
  3. Infection
  4. Malignancy
  5. Mortality

Principles of management:

  1. Treat reversible causes : prevent episodes of acute on chronic kidney disease. (UTIs, nephrotoxic drugs, low renal perfusion)
  2. Slow the progression of kidney disease by
    1. Treat the underlying cause
    2. Blood pressure control - ACEi / ARB
    3. Management of proteinuria - SGLT-2 inhibitors
    4. Other measures: protein restriction - normal protein diet, smoking cessation, treatment of metabolic acidosis, glycaemic control.
  3. Medical management of the complications of CKD
  4. Adjusting drug doses as appropriate to GFR
  5. Preparation for kidney replacement therapy.

Management of puerperal psychosis:

  • Delusional perception about the baby leading to ideas of infaticide can occur.
  • Inpatient treatment is best as a specialized mother and baby unit.
  • Mother and baby contact continues but must be supervised.
  • Pharmacotherapy: Antipsychotics, antidepressants, Lithium for mania (in which case breastfeeding should be avoided)
  • ECT if poor symptoms resolution (it's rapidly effective)
