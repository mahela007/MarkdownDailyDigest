Mnemonic: 3 Rules of dynamic auscultation

  1. Increased preload increases left sided murmurs (except VSD) (and decreases HOCM and MVP).
  2. Increased afterload increases the left sided regurgitant murmurs (AR, MR) and VSD and (decreases HOCM and MVP).
  3. Increasing afterload OR preload decreases HOCM and MVP murmur.
  4. And of course, the inverse of these is true as well.

[!INFO] pituitary apoplexy

  • Sudden bleed into the pituitary gland.
  • Due to bleeding into an adenoma. see [[#Sellar masses]]
  • can cause sudden, excruciating headache, diplopia from pressure on CNIII, and hypopituitarism.
    • eg: patient with galactrorrhea presenting with sudden onset headache -> suspect apoplexy
  • Clinical evidence of hormone deficiency is less commonly found but biochemical tests do show releasing hormone deficiencies. (ACTH, TSH, FSH, LH etc)
  • Symptoms can resolve spontaneously over weeks due to reabsorption but surgical decompression may be required.
  • In adults, causes are the same as causes of [[Hormone Physiology#Hypopituitarism|hypopituitarism]].
  • In children, GH deficiency can be caused by various inherited causes as well. (Acquired causes are same as in adults)
Diagnosis of growth hormone deficiency
  • GH deficiency is usually diagnosed in children.
  • It's suspected due to short stature.
  • First rule out chronic systemic disease, hypothyroidism, Turner syndrome in girls, and skeletal disorders using history and examination.
  • Labs to check for GH deficiency:
    • IGF-1 levels will be low.
    • Low "bone age"
  • Growth hormone stimulation tests are used as confirmatory tests but should only be interpreted in the context of clinical picture because the test "has shortcomings". The confirmatory test is not even necessary if other clinical features can support the diagnosis.-UpToDate
  • Pharmacologic stimulants used in the test are Clonidine, Arginine, Glucagon, Insulin induced hypoglycaemia
    • Glucagon causes hyperglycaemia and reflex insulin mediated hypoglycaemia which griggers GH release. Safer than direct insulin mediated hyperglycae.
    • Insulin mediated test is very potent.
      Causes of Increased GH presenting as gigantism (i.e ↑ GH before epiphyseal fusion)
      • McCune-albright syndrome - diagnosed in young adults
      • Carney complex - presents in middle aged people
      • Neurofibromatosis

Sellar masses

  • Commonest sellar masses in adults are pituitary adenomas.
  • most common of these are lactotroph adenomas
  • Followed by non-functioning adenomas
  • and then somatotroph adenomas.
    Source
  • Symptoms: fever, ?rash chills, rigors, tachycardia, hypotension.
  • Antibiotics can then be reinstated.
  • Hypotension can become life threatening.
  • Pathogenesis unknown -> possibly immune reaction to lysed spirochetal cells.
  • "Left shift" : ill defined term meaning increased band forms, myelocytes and metamyelocytes. -> indicates infection, sepsis or chronic myeloid leukemia.
  • Myelocytes are neutrophil precursors seen mainly in bone marrow. Greek "meta" means 'after'. Metamyelocytes are their descendants, and can be seen in the blood.
  • Band forms are immature neutrophils whose nucleus hasn't yet become segmented and have a C or S shape.
  • Neutrophilia due to steroid use does not show a left shift.

[!INFO] Liddle syndrome (aka Pseudohyperaldosteronism)
Gain of function mutation of ENaC Channels -> Sodium retention + Hypertension + Hypokalemia + metabolic alkalosis.
#autosomalDominant
Treated with amiloride or triamterine, low sodium diet.
Spironolactone won't work because as an aldosterone antagonist, it won't affect the activity of the mutated ENaC channels.
No SHAME in going to Lidl
Sodium channel, Hypokalemia, Amiloride, Mutation, Early onset hypertension
Also has low renin level

Mostly reabsorbed in PCT (actively by Na/K ATPase) . *Secreted in DCT and CD *

The pathogenesis of RA involves a complex interplay between B cells, T cells, and dendritic cells. A multitude of environmental and genetic factors leads to loss of tolerance to the proteins that have a citrulline residue, which leads to the production of autoantibodies like anti-cyclic citrullinated protein/peptide antibody (ACPA) and RF. - Source
Interaction of macrophages + T + B cells -> Over production of TNF-alpha -> recruitment of inflammatory cells + stimulation of osteoclasts -> synovitis and joint destruction.

  • Rheumatoid factors (IgM against Fc portion of IgG) are found in 80% of RA patients.

Lyme disease

[!TIP] Mnemonic: Attacks conduction tissue

  • Borrelia burgdoferi - spirochete.

Has early and late features

Early features:

  • Pincu - 3 to 32 days
  • Earliest features is erythema migrans - expands to form large red annular lesions. ("Bull's eye rash - present in 80%)
  • Then disseminates via haematogenous dissemination to various organs (heart and CNS) (patient then gets headache, neck stiff ness, arthralgia, malaise)
  • Affects CNS -> meningeal irriration
  • Encephalitic signs -> cranial nerve signs (Can mimick Bell's palsy)
  • Can have bilateral facial nerve palsy
  • Peripheral neuropathy
  • Mono neuritis multiplex
  • Myelitis
  • Cerebellar ataxia.

Late features of infections: (after 30 days)

  • Cardiac - atrioventricular block
    • CHB or IIry Wenckeback,
    • also, LV dysfunction, cardiomegaly
  • Chronic neurologic involvement - subtle encephalopahty, axonal polyneuropathy,
  • Post lyme syndrome:
    • Pain, neurocognitive manifestations, fatigue lasting for years.
  • Culture from erythema migrans biopsy
  • Borellia burgodoferi PCR in joint aspirate
  • IgM ang IgG by Elisa and western blot.
    Oral : <- used for early disease
  • doxycycline for non pregnant females
  • Children : amoxicillin
  • 3rd line : cefuroxime and erythromycin

IV:

  • Ceftriaxone is first line for disseminated disease or CNS involvement
  • Cefotaxime is also an option.
  • [[General Medicine 1#Jarisch-Herxheimer reaction|Jarisch-Herxheimer reaction]] may be seen after first dose of antibiotics.
