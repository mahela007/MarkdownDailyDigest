Intro:

The kidneys must

  1. excrete acid in the amount equivalent to the production of non volatile acids in the body. (which varies with diet, disease etc)
  2. and reabsorb filtered bicarbonate.
    Both processes are linked to the nephrons ability to secrete H+ ions into the filtrate.

Mechanisms of secretion of H+

  • Proximal tubule - H+ is secreted in exchange for Na+ by the NHE3 (sodium hydrogen exchange) by secondary active trasnport.
  • Distal tubule: H+ secretion is independent of Na. Secretion is driven by H+ ATPase pump. (primary active transport)
    • In acidosis, more H+ ATPases are inserted into the apical membrane -> more H+ pumped out.
    • Aldoesterone increases action of this pump.
    • In the DCT, there is also a H+/K+ pump (K in H out)

ECG T waves

Normal T wave

  • Positive in most leads; Should be concordant with the QRS.
  • Tallest in V2-V3.
  • Asymmetric : descending limb is steeper than the ascending limb.
  • Amplitude diminishes with age.

Normal T wave inversions

Isolated T inversion in V1 (common, concordant with QRS) OR V2 OR III or aVL is normal.
T inversion in two contiguous leads it pathological.
