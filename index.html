07/04/2024
There are many elderly females who present with exacerbations of cough who have an underlying diagnosis of bronchiectasis.

Pathology

Pathologic Definition:

"persistent and lifelong widening of the bronchial airways and weakening of the function mucociliary transport mechanism owing to repeated infection contributing to bacterial invasion and mucus pooling throughout the bronchial tree". Sourcd

Pathologic mechanisms:

  • recurrent infections, airway obstruction, and peribronchial fibrosis.Source


Source

Clinical aspects

Typical presentation:

Source

  1. Recurrent chest infections
  2. Productive cough >= 8 weeks (on "most days of the week" - UpToDate)
  3. Copious amounts of sputum
  4. Haemoptysis
  5. History of exacerbations

Typical background history:

  • History of recurrent pulmonary infections over several years.
  • haemopytis
  • Fatigue
  • Weight loss

[!INFO] Relative Frequency of symptoms:
Cough: 98%, sputum: 78% (sputum is typically mucoid and relatively odorless), dyspnea: 62%, haemoptysis: 56% to 92%, and pleuritic chest pain: 20% (secondary to chronic coughing).

Physical findings: Source

  • 75% bibasal crackles - Course
  • 22% have wheezing

Features of exacerbation of bronchiectasis:

  • Change in sputum production,
  • increased dyspnoea,
  • increased cough,
  • fever,
  • increased wheezing,
  • and reduced pulmonary function.

Main causes:

  • causal role for cigarette smoking in bronchiectasis has not been demonstrated - UpToDate
  • [[General Medicine 1#Allergic bronchopulmonary aspergillosis|ABPA]]
  • Mycobacterial infection
  • Other virulent pneumonias - staph aureus
  • Childhood respiratory infections
  • Rheumamtic diseases:
    • Rheumamtoid arthritis and Sjogrens
  • [[passMedicine Summaries#Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency|alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency]]
  • Cystic fibrosis and young syndrome
  • Immunosuppresion
  • Airway obstruction

Distribution

The distribution of bronchiectasis can help in narrowing the differential diagnosis. Lower lobe bronchiectasis is the commonest zonal predilection in bronchiectasis.
Source

Causes for upper lobe bronchiectasis:

  • Cystic fibrosis
  • Tuberculosis

Management

Source

Treatment Goals:

  1. Identifying and treating the underlying cause
    1. If it is reversible / treatable
  2. Improve tracheobronchial clearance
    1. Do postural drainage
    2. Examples: Source-Video, Source-Video
      1. Different positions are used based on the affected lobe.
  3. Control infection
    1. Pseudomonas and H influenza require special consideration.
  4. Reverse airflow obstruction
    1. Inhaled bronchodilators

"Most physicians recommend mucus clearance as the mainstay of therapy in bronchiectasis"
