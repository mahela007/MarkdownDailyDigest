Mass reflex

Occurs in quadriplegic patients.
There is extreme lowering of the threshold for the withdrawal reflex. Minor stimuli can trigger disproportionate reactions like prolonged withdrawal and even flexion-extension patterns in the other limbs; Aberrant reinnervation followings spinal cord injury can cause minor stimuli to cause autonomic dysregulation (hypotension, pallor, sweating) and bladder / bowel incontinence.

However, according to Robbins, OA is primarily a 'degenerative process' rather than an inflammatory one.

  • Chondrocytes responds to stress in such as way that cartilage matrix is broken down.
  • There is vertical and horizontal fibrillation and cracking of the matrix.
  • This is known as chondromalacia; the articular surface has become soft and granular (?instead of shiny and glassy)
  • Subchondral bone is then exposed and polished by friction -> bone eburnation; look like ivory! (mnemonic; 'Ebur' means ivory)
  • Pieces of bone and cartilage are disloged into the sinovial fluid - synovial 'mice'.
  • Osteophytes develop at the margin of the articular surface
  • Synovial fluid cysts occur in the subchondral bone.
  • Synovial can proliferate in severe cases to form a pannus.
