Muscle spindle

Source-YouTube <- a good overview


Muscle spindles are organs that sense muscle stretch. They are embedded in muscle, alongside contractile 'extrafusal' muscle fibers.
The 'intrafusal' sensory fibers of the muscle spindle consist of

  • bag fibers and
  • chain fibers
    Innervation:
  • Bag fibers and chain fibers are both innervated by Type 1 neurons -> Group Ia = annulospiral. They are naked neuron endings wrapping around the central parts of the intrafusal fibers.
  • Chain fibers are innervated by Type 2 neurons as well. -> Group II = "flower spray" neurons. They innervate the distal ends of the chain fibers.
  • Motor efferents: find gamma fibers innervate the contractile areas of the intrafusal fibers described below.

The afferents (Group Ia and Group II) have slow adapting and fast adapting fibers which sense absolute length and rate of change of length, respectively.

The ends of the chain fibers contain contractile areas which 'take up the slack' when the muscle contracts, to keep the chain fiber taught.

The lengthening and shortening of muscles is detected by the muscle spindles.
The tension of the muscle is detected by the tendon spindles. (Golgi tendon organ)
