Iron absorption and regulation


See [[2021-Basic Sciences#Abnormal iron metabolism]] <- dysregulated iron absorption

Hepcidin is a key hormone in iron regulation.
It regulated iron release from enterocytes and macrophages.
It also has antimicrobial activity.
Produced by the liver.
Decreased hepcidin production is stimulated by

  • Hypoxia, anaemia, erythropoietic expansion, iron deficiency, ineffetive erythropoiesis (thalassemia)
    Increased production (and decreased iron absorption) are stimulated by
  • increased iron levels
  • inflammation
  • infection, endotoxin

Haemochromatosis (HH)

#autosomal-Recessive

Pathogenesis

Caused by mutations (C282Y variant) of the HFE gene.
Increased iron intestinal iron absorption.
Haemochromatosis = iron overload from any cause.
Hereditary haemochromatosis = the inherited disease causing iron overload.

[!INFO] C282Y is necessary but not sufficient for haemochromatosis
Only 20% of C282Y homozygotes actually develop haemochromatosis. In fact, this mutation is quite common in the population.

HH is said to be a low penetrance disease:
HH is now known to be more common than was previously though because of the advent of genetic screening. But many people who carry the mutation are asymptomatic.

Abnormal iron metabolism

  • C282Y causes inappropriately low Hepcidin.
    • High Hepcidin reduces iron deposition in RET. Therefore, in HH, iron deposition occurs first in parenchymal tissue and then in RET. (oppositve of transfusional iron overload)
  • Low hepcidin causes increased intestinal absorption of iron.
  • negative feedback inhibition of iron absorption is disrupted in HH.
  • Significant iron accumulation takes decades.
  • Iron accumulation occurs mostly in LHPP (Liver, heart, pancreas and pituitary).

Clinical features - when to suspect

  • Most common presentation nowadays is elevated ferritin.
  • Males present around 40; females present after menopause.
  • Symptoms develop because of iron deposition in organs:
    • LFT abnormality - 75%
    • Weakness and lethargy - 75%
    • Skin Hyperpigmentation - 70%
    • DM - 48%
    • Arthrlagia
    • Impotence in males
    • ECG abnormalities
