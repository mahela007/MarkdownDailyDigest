Introduction to liver masses

  • The liver and lungs are the organs most commonly involved in metastasis.
  • Most liver cancers are metastatic deposits. (from colon, lung and breast)
  • The vast majority of 'intrinsic' liver cancers are hepatocellular carcinomas.
    • Two other types (hepatoblastoma and angiosarcoma are very very rare)
  • The most common benign lesions are cavernous hemangiomas
    • Soft, <2cm nodules.
    • Can be mistaken for metastatic deposits.
    • But biopsy can cause catastrophic bleeding!
  • Another benign lesions is Focal nodular hyperplasia
    • Seen most often in women of reproductive age.
    • Non neoplastic nodules which form around intrahepatic vascular abnormalities.
    • Poorly capsulated
    • Growth stimulated by oestrogen
  • the 3rd benign lesion is hepatic adenoma
    • No malignant potential but they can rupture causing catastrophic intra-abdominal haemorrhage.
    • Uncapsulated
    • Growth stimulated by oestrogen

Fortunately, most liver lesions have a characteristics enhancement pattern on contrast imaging.

"A hepatocellular carcinoma, a highly vascular primary lesion, will demonstrate hyperenhancement in the arterial phase and venous or delayed phase washout whilst a hemangioma should match the blood pool in each phase (same as the aorta in arterial etc)." - Source

Hepatocellular carcinomas

[!TIP] SUMMARY:
Occurs in chronic liver disease
Aggressive
Typically diagnosed late. (Median survival 6-20 months)
Difficult to treat -> high rate of recurrence

  • 75 percent of primary liver tumors are HCC
  • cholangiocarcinoma remaining 25%
    Potentially curative:
  • Resection - curative in eligible patients; tumour should not show vascular invasion to be resectable.
  • Thermal ablation - similar eligibility criteria to resection, but resection generally preferred.
  • Liver transplantation - For patients who are not surgical candidates due to underlying liver disease.
    Palliative:
  • Locoregional liver directed therapy - isolated, unresectable HCC who are ineligible for transplantation and who have a limited intrahepatic tumor burden (TARE, TACE, thermal ablation)
