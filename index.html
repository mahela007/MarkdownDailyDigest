Renal impairment in gout:

Two main mechanisms : nephrolithiasis and chronic urate nephropathy;
So management of gout with concurrent renal impairment is a clinical issue.

Treatment

Treatment of acute flares:

  • NSAIDS - very effective but contraindicated in renal impairment.
  • If NSAIDs can't be used, colchicine is effective.
  • Colchicine - alternative to NSAIDS. Inhibits neutrophil microtubules, thereby impairing neutrophil migration into sites of inflammation.
  • Corticosteroids - oral prednisolone or IM or intra-articular depot methylprednisolone.

Control measures:

Goal is to lower and maintain urate levels < 360 micromol/L.

Diet: Important because it will reduce urate levels by 15%.

  • reduce alcohol - specially beer, avoid high fructose foods (like soft drinks)
  • Reduce total calorie and cholesterol intake.
  • Avoid red meat, spinach.

Urate lowering medication

  • First few episodes of gout are managed symptomatically as above.
  • Urate lowering therapy is started when episodes become frequent and problematic.
  • Allopurinol:
    • Only when attacks are frequent and problematic or there is associated renal impairment. Side effects : GI disturbances, blood dyscrasias and rashes.
    • Xanthine oxidase inhibitors -> rapidly lowers urate levels.
    • (do not start within 6 months of an attack; acute lowering of serum urate concentration can itself trigger gout episodes).
  • Febuxostat: as effective as allopurinol but overall associated with higher all cause mortality! 😲
    • Therefore, second line drug when allopurinol isn't tolerated.
  • Pegloticase:
    • Given IV for severe gout.
  • Uricosuric agents:
    • Probenecid - avoid in renal impairment
  • Losartan -
    • Also has uricorusic properties.
  • Targeted therapies:
    • Anakinra - IL-1 inhibitor; very expensive.
    • canakinumab - IL-1beta inhibitor.

Chronic tophaceous gout

Persistent inflammation.
Skin and periarticular urate crystal deposits (tophi) causing chronic pain and overlying skin ulceration.
Punched out bone cysts on X ray.
Urate nephropathy with renal impairment.
Treatment:

  • It's associated with chronic diuretic use -> stop or change diuretics.
  • allopurinol
  • pegloticase (especially in chemotherapy)

Supply of the sinoatrial node:

  • In 60%, supplied by the right coronary artery.
  • In 40%, supplied by the left coronary artery.

About 10% : left dominant

Contraindications

Sacubitril-valsartan and AND ACE inhibitors can cause angioedema via their inhibitory effect on the kallikrein system, which increases bradykinin. Both are contraindicated if the patient has a history of angiooedema of any cause.
Both are contraindicated in pregnancy due to teratogenicity.

Cause of death in heart failure

Possible causes are

  1. Progressive pump failure
  2. Unexpected sudden cardiac death: usually from a ventricular arrhythmia
  3. SCD in the setting of worsening heart failure.

[!INFO] Mode of death in heart failure
Sudden cardiac death is commoner in NHYA II and III but worsening pump failure is the commonest in class IV.
