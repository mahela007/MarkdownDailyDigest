#2021GM-JUL/Q25

Strategies are different for mechanical valves and bioprosthetic valves.
Source

  • Depends on the which valve is replaced, which type of mechanical valve is used and presence of other thromboembolic risk factors.
  • All mechanical valves require lifelong anticoagulation with Vitamin K agonist.
  • There is now ❌NO RECOMMENDATION❌ for giving antiplatelets along with VKAs for thromboprophylaxis with mechanical valves.
  • So far, the only option for anticoagulation with mechanical heart valves is warfarin.
  • Target INR is 2.5 for 3 to 6 months if there are no other reasons to anticoagulate using a VKA.
    • This is because the first 90-180 days are considered high risk.
  • All patients (with no other indication for anticoagulation) get aspirin after the period of anticoagulation.

[!TIP] Only indication for intra-venous vitamin K (and PCC) is

  • Life threatening bleeding

#2021BSQ-NOV/Q36

'Special' in SVE means 'innervation of branchial arch derived muscles'

  • SVE: Motor efferents to facial muscles
    • and Posterior auricular nerve and branch to posterior belly of digastric and stylohyoid muscle, facial expression, stapedius muscle
  • GVE: Parasympathetic efferents lacrimal glands, submandibular glands and sublingual glands (but not the parotid gland (that's by glossopharyngeal nerve) - via the greater petrosal nerve and chorda tympani
  • SVA: Afferent taste fibers from front 2/3 of the tongue - via the chorda tympani
  • GSA:*Somatic afferents from the pinna and external auditory canal.
  • Bell's palsy is defined as 'idiopathic' isolated facial nerve palsy but a viral (HSV) aetiology is suspected.
    • There is swelling with oedema and inflammatory infiltrate. Damage is greatest in the narrowest part of the passage: the labyrinthine part.
  • But there is a x3 risk during T3 of #pregnancy and week 1 post partum.
    Basis: swelling of the nerve within the confined petrosal canal due to viral infection or reactivation. (HSV)
  • Symptoms: facial nerve weakness occurring over 1 - 2 days. Hyperacusis, loss of taste of anterior 2/3 of tongue, pain behind the ear, ipsilateral decreased tearing or salivation. There will be no other systemic signs (eg no rashes or fever).
  • Maximum weakness is reached within 3 weeks and then improves. Some improvement is always seen within 4 months of onset
  • During recovery "crocodile tears" syndrome can occur. AKA "Gusto-lacrimal" reflex. Patients produce tears when eating or drinking.
    • Crocodiles are said to weep after killing their prey. Source
    • Mechanism uncertain; may be due to regenerative fiber or aberrant synapse formation innervating the lacrimal gland.
  • HIV seroconversion is the most common cause in africa. (because it causes mononeuropathies)
  • Glucocorticoids within 3 day of onset shortens symptom duration.
  • Antivirals are given (specially if weakness if severe) but there is no proven benefit.
  • Synkinesia occurs in 15%. (Involuntary contraction of some facial muscles during voluntary movement of other facial muscles) (can be treated with botulinum toxin)
  • There is a 7% recurrence risk.
