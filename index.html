Physiologic haematologic changes in pregnancy

  • Haemoglobin

  • iron deficiency is frequent.

  • Folate deficiency:

  • Vitamin B12 levels fall slightly in pregnancy but there is no deficiency. Levels but recover post partum.

  • Platelet counts fall by 10% of baseline.

    • Probably due to platelet sequestration in splenic and placental circulation.
    • Platelet counts drop progressively throughout pregnancy and rise after delivery.
    • But counts still remain within the normal range in uncomplicated pregnancies.
    • Counts within 150,000 to 100,000 range are likely to be [[#Incidental thrombocytopaenia of pregnancy]].
    • However, this physiological thrombocytopaenia can be severe in 7% of pregnant women. Should look for other causes if especially if Plt count is < 100,000.
    • See [[#Thrombocytopaenia in pregnancy]] below

Thrombocytopaenia in pregnancy

  • Define as platelets < 150,000.
  • There can be many causes. Commonest is incidental thrombocytopaenia of pregnancy.
  • 75% - the cause is idiopathic and with mild thrombocytopaenia. - [[#Incidental thrombocytopaenia of pregnancy]]
  • 21% - secondary to hypertensive disorder
  • 4% - immune thrombocytopaenic purpura.

Incidental thrombocytopaenia of pregnancy

  • Also called gestational thrombocytopaenia (GT)
  • Diagnosis of exclusion
  • Detected at time of delivery
  • Count is greater than 70k. (usually between 150k to 100k)
  • Recovers in 6 weeks.
  • No treatment needed.
  • But thrombocytopaenia can occur in [[Hypertension in pregnancy|Hypertensive disorders of pregnancy]]
