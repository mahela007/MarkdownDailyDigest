Antifolate drugs

  • Dietary folates are inactive; They must be converted to active tetrahydrofolate by the action of dihydrofolate reductase.
  • Some antifolate drugs like methotrexate, triamterene, pyrimethamine, trimethoprim inhibit dihydrofolate reductase, thus blocking folate action.
  • Other drugs which inhibit folate action do so by different unknown mechanisms.
    • Anticonvulsants (phenytoin (PHT), carbamazepine (CBZ), or barbiturates)
    • Anti tuberculosis drugs (INH, Ethambuotl and pyrazinamide, ?not rifampicin)
    • Oral contraceptives
    • Alcohol
    • Sulfonamides DO NOT inhibit human folate metabolism. [[antibiotics|Sulphonamides]]
  • To counteract the inhibitory effects of methotrexate, oral folate supplements are given.
    • Folic acid is the synthetic form of dietary folates but it also needs to be activated by dihydrofolate reductase.
    • For high dose MTX treatment, folinic acid, a reduced form of folate is given. This bypasses the need for dihydrofolate reductase.
    • In addition, Folinic acid potentiates the action of 5-fluorouracil, an anti cancer drug used in colon cancer (among others)

[!INFO] Requirement for dihydrofolate reductase
Dietary folate and folic acid require this enzyme to be activated.
Folinic acid does not require DHF reductase.

Uric acid nephropathy

Uric acid is insoluble. Urate is slightly more soluble. Therefore, precipitation is increased in acidic environments. Humans do not have the uricase enzyme which converts urate to soluble allantoin.
There are 3 forms of uric acid induced nephropathy:

Source

  1. Acute uric acid nephropathy
    1. Occurs in background of leukaemia and lymphoma. Massive increased purine metabolism causes uric acid to precipitate in the distal nephron causing obstruction and compression of nearby vessels leading to oliguric AKI.
  2. Chronic urate nephropathy
    1. Some controversy about the existence of this entry.
    2. Pathophysiology is the formation of micro tophi of monosodium urate which then sets up chronic inflammation and granulomas.
  3. Urate stones
