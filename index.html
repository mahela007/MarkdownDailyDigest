  • Immune complex mediated, small vessel vasculitis.
  • Prototype disease of "diffuse alveolar haemorrhage syndromes".
  • Diffuse alveolar haemorrhage syndromes consist of a triad of haemoptysis, anaemia and diffuse pulmonary infiltrates.
  • Respiratory symptoms may precede glomerulonephritis by weeks to months.
  • Mild to life threatening haemopsytis.
    Treatment: remove the antibodies by plasmapheresis and prevent recurrence with immunosuppression (prednisilone / cyclophosphamide)
    Prognosis: Recurrence is rare; earlier diagnosis -> less severe renal failure -> recovery; late diagnosis or significant renal impairment at presentation -> chronic kidney disease and dialysis requirement.
