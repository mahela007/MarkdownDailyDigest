Acute screening of HIV infection

[!TIP] Summary
Combined antigen + antibody tests are preferred as they have a shorter window period of about 4 weeks.

  • eg: patient presents with inguinal lymphadenopathy 1 month after unprotected sexual intercourse.
  • Screening is now done with combined p24 antigen + Antibody test. (i.e "fourth generation" tests)
  • Antibody test alone may not the sensitive enough within 6 weeks after exposure.
  • Antigen tests become positive 2 - 3 weeks after exposure.
  • However, most rapid tests are antibody tests. They can begin detecting HIV from 18-90 days after exposure.

[!INFO] What is a window period?
Source
The time taken from exposure to the test becoming positive for a given percentage of positive patients.

  • By 6 weeks, 99% of combined antigen/antibody tests for HIV will be +ve. If negative at this stage, there's no need to for repeat testing. (and at 4 weeks, 95%)
  • In contrast, the antigen test alone is positive in 99% at only 60 days. (roughly 2 months)
  • Negative test results done within the window period must be repeated after the window period elapses.

Chronic phase
