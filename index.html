[!INFO] Renin and aldosterone are increased in #pregnancy
Pregnancy specific ranges are required for evaluation
Spironolactone - used to treat Conn's syndrome - is contraindicated in pregnancy because as an antiandrogen it can cause feminization of a male foetus.


Conn syndrome was named after J. W. Conn who first described it in 1955, in a patient who had hypertension with an aldosterone-producing adenoma.
Classic Conn's syndrome = adrenal adenoma.
The adenoma is characterized by

  • increased aldosterone secretion from the adrenal glands,
  • suppressed plasma renin,
  • hypertension,
  • and hypokalemia.

Bilateral adrenal hyperplasia is actually a much commoner cause of hyperaldosteronism than Conn's syndrome.

Diagnosis of Conn's syndrome

Elevated ARR (aldosterone renin ratio) is a screening test:

  • Some people who have elevated ARR will suppress aldosterone appropriately when given a saline load excluding a diagnosis of Conn's.
  • ACE inhibitors can raise renin and lower aldosterone levels, thereby causing false-negative ARR results; Source
  • Confirmatory test: Inadequate suppression of aldosterone following infusion of 2L of N/S over 4 hours.

Graves' disease

  • Common cause of hyperthyroidism

  • Commoner in women - 30 - 60 years.

  • TSH-Receptor antibodies (thyrotropin receptor or TRAb) stimulate thyroid gland to make thyroid hormones. -> diffuse, non tender, goiter

  • Graves eye disease is a prominent feature.

  • Disease follows a relapsing remitting course. Majority have recurrences.

  • Associated with other autoimmune diseases like myasthenia, vitiligo, pernicious anaemia

  • Patients will usually eventually become hypothyroid.

  • Orbitopathy and pretibial myxoedema are not related to thyroxine levels.

Meningitis and Encephalitis

#2022SBR-MAY Q19

[!INFO] The main difference between meningitis and ecephalitis:
In encephalitis, there is presence of abnormal brain function.

  • eg : altered mental status, motor or sensory deficits, altered behaviour, personality changes, speech and movement disorders
  • but NOT seizures - they can occur in meningitis as well.

Viral infection can cause encephalitis because of

  1. Primary infection
  2. Postinfectious encephalitis - ADEM (acute disseminated encephalomyelitis) which is an immune mediated condition.

West Nile Virus

1% of infected people develop severe symptoms. More severe disease is seen in people over 50. Mortality rate is about 10% in severe disease.
Mild symptoms : constitutional, Rash
Severe symptoms: Severe muscle weakness, flaccid paralysis, erythematous maculopapular or morbilliform rash on neck, trunk arms or legs.

It can be confused with Guillain barre syndrome.

[!INFO] Maculopapular rash with neurologic symptoms above is highly suggestive.
The rash isn't seen in other viral encepahlitides.

Tumour lysis syndrome

#2022SBR-MAY

Occurs with treatment of lymphoproliferative disorders.
Can occur spotaneously!
Commonest associated cancers are Non-hodgkin lymphoma and leukemia.
Triggers: Chemotherapy or steroids
Tumour cell death -> release of intracellular components ->

  1. Hyperkalemia
  2. Lysed cells release nucleic acids -> metabolised to hypoxanthine -> xanthine -> uriac acid -> very high uric acid levels -> Urate crystal nephropathy -> AKI -> hyperkalemia
  3. Hyperphosphatemia
  4. Hypocalcemia <- calcium phosphate precipitation due to hyperphosphatemia. [[Hormone Physiology#Phosphate and calcium]]
  5. Elevated LDH

Cause of death: cardiac arrhythmias, seizures.

Treatment: prophylactic prehydration, allopurinol (xanthine oxidase inhibitor and Rasburicase - recombinant urase oxidase)
