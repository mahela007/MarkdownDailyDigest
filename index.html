Meningioma

#2017GM-OCT/Q18

  • Benign, slow growing tumours arising from the arachnoid.
  • Can cause local bone erosions.
  • Psammoma bodies (Form of dystrophic calcification) are present on histology.
  • Rare below the tentorium.
  • Common sites: parasagittal, pituitary fossa etc.
  • Arise from ECL cells in the GI system and bronchopulmonary systems. ^543118
  • Also called APUDOMAs
  • Majority occurs in the GI system, particularly in the small intestine.

The most common biologically active substance secreted from carcinoid tumors is 5-HT (serotonin), a vasoactive peptide whose biosynthesis is accomplished nearly exclusively by the enterochromaffin cells. Synthesized from the amino acid tryptophan, the release of 5-HT into the systemic circulation can cause the classic symptoms of the carcinoid syndrome, which include diarrhea, episodic flushing, bronchoconstriction, and eventual right-sided valvular heart disease (and probably oedema from right heart failure?)

Trigeminal neuralgia

  • "neuralgia" not "cephalgia"
  • Classified as "facial pain".
  • Electric, shock like pain lasting seconds
  • Occurs in elderly, hypertension is the main risk factor.
  • Aberrant vascular loop causes compression of trigenimal nerve.
  • ?no autonomic trigeminal symptoms
  • Spontaneous remission occurs lasting months but disease invariably recurs
  • Treatment: Carbamazepine, surgical treatment is second line but more effective. (RF ablation)
