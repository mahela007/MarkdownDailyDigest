5. Dietary sodium restriction (and hyponatremia in general) increases renin secretion

Causes of falsely elevated creatinine

Elevation of S.Cr. usually indicated a reduction of the GFR.
But this is not always the case, due to

  • Increased production of S.Cr.

  • Interference with Assay - Jaffe reaction; quantifies color to detect S.Cr level

  • Decreased tubular secretion of S.cr
    Renal blood flow is related to renal plasma flow by haematocrit.
    GFR is the amount of filtrate formed per minute.
    Measured using a substance that is freely filtered and neither secreted or reabsorbed.
    GFR in an adult male is 125 mL/min. It correlated well with surface area.
    So to compare GFR of two people, we compare GFR per unit surface area.
    Clearance : the Plasma Volume from which a substance is completely removed in a given time. Clinically estimated using Serum creatinine.

    Theoretically, clearance should be measured using a substance which is freely filtered, not secreted and not reabsorbed. The ideal substance is inulin.
    In practice creatinine is used because it is an endogenous substance. But creatinine is secreted in small amounts by the tubules. Therefore, using creatinine as the molecule slightly overestimates the GFR.
    Accurately measuring creatinine clearance would require measuring urine creatinine. So for convenience, we measure the serum creatinine and approximate the GFR based on that.

    This is done using fancy formulas such as MDRD formula or the newer CKD-EPI formula which account for age, sex, race etc when producing an eGFR value. Source

Examples: Clearance of glucose is 0 ml/min, clearance of creatinine is 140 mL/min and clearance of PAH is 560 ml/min
