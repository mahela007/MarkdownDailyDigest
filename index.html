Release of a public officer

Release to another post

To be appointed to another post in the public service, officer must be release dfrom current post.
Application for release must be forwarded to appointing authority through head of depatment or head of institution

The request will be denied only if

  1. Officer is under a bond to serve in the current post
  2. officer has special training which is required for his current post
  3. Release would significantly disrupt the functioning of the department to an extent greater than the officer actually serving in the current post.

If an officer has received special training for his current post, he should be released only if this training is useful for his new post.

If selection to a new post requires training, officer will be relased for training

Appeals against transfer

Appeals against annual transfer

Officer should submit appeals through head of Dept. or head of institution to PSC.
Appeal should be made within 14 days of when the order was made public.
Must be made in the stipulated format - appendix 16.
Appeal and supporting documents should be forwarded to the PSC before the 15th of November
Decision to be reached by PSC in within 15 days of receipt of an appeal.

Appeals against non-annual transfer and promotions

Appeal is to the commission
Strictly according to format.
Submitted through an authority with delegated power.
Appeal against a promotion : within 30 days.
Appeal against a transfer: 14 days.
The HOI, HOD should send the appeal so that it reaches PSC within 15 days.
If agrieved by PSC decsion, can appeal with Administravel appeals tribunal.

Functions of the secretary to the ministry

In the Sri Lankan government, the Secretary to the Ministry plays a crucial role in the administrative hierarchy. The Secretary is the highest-ranking civil servant within a particular ministry and acts as the principal advisor to the Minister. Their primary responsibility is to assist the Minister in formulating and implementing government policies and programs within their respective ministries.

The specific duties and functions of the Secretary to the Ministry include:

  1. Administrative Leadership: The Secretary provides overall administrative leadership and management within the ministry. They oversee the day-to-day operations, coordinate activities, and ensure effective functioning of the ministry.

  2. Policy Development: The Secretary assists the Minister in formulating policies, strategies, and plans related to the ministry's functions and responsibilities. They conduct research, gather information, analyze data, and provide recommendations to the Minister on policy matters.

  3. Inter-Ministerial Coordination: The Secretary coordinates and liaises with other ministries, government departments, and agencies to ensure effective collaboration and implementation of cross-cutting policies and programs. They facilitate communication and cooperation between different entities within the government.

  4. Budgeting and Financial Management: The Secretary is responsible for budget preparation and financial management within the ministry. They work closely with the Minister and relevant stakeholders to allocate resources, monitor expenditures, and ensure transparency and accountability in financial matters.

  5. Implementation and Monitoring: The Secretary oversees the implementation of policies, programs, and projects within the ministry. They monitor progress, evaluate outcomes, and make necessary adjustments to ensure effective delivery of services and achievement of objectives.

  6. Communication and Public Relations: The Secretary represents the ministry in various forums, meetings, and negotiations. They engage with stakeholders, communicate government policies and decisions, and address public concerns related to the ministry's functions.

  7. Legal Compliance: The Secretary ensures that the ministry operates in accordance with relevant laws, regulations, and procedures. They provide legal advice, review documents, and facilitate legal compliance within the ministry.

Overall, the Secretary to the Ministry plays a pivotal role in supporting the Minister and facilitating the smooth functioning of the ministry. They contribute to policy formulation, administrative management, inter-ministerial coordination, and effective implementation of government initiatives.
