[!INFO] Hypokalemia periodic paralysis

  • Caused by a mutation in skeletal muscle calcium channels
  • Normal skeletal muscle becomes hyperexcitable during hypokalemia.
  • However, in HPP, skeletal muscle becomes unexcitable during hypokalemia.
  • Therefore, patients develop acute episodes of muscle weakness.
  • Inheritance: #autosomalDominant with variable penetrance.
  • Typical history: patient develops acute paralysis (lower limb > upper limb, areflexia) following carbohydrate heavy meal or heavy exercise.
  • Women more affected than men.
    Source

[!INFO] Hyperkalemic periodic paralysis
problem in the sodium channels of muscles.
K+ shifts extracellularly
Causes muscle weakness or muscle rigidity
Rare than Hypokalemic paralysis
Affects men and women equally.
Short episodes of muscle paralysis.
Frequency increases until 40 years old and then decreases.
Triggers: K+ rich foods, exercise.
