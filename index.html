Glucocorticoids

  • Reduce the size and lymphoid content of lymph nodes and spleen.
  • but no toxic effect on myeloid or erythroid precursors
  • Cell mediated immunity is affected more than humoral immunity.
  • but antibody production is also reduced.
  • Continous administration increases the rate of IgG catabolism.
  • Used for diverse indications including immunosuppresion in organ transplant recipients. (solid organ and haematopoietic)
    Cyclophosphamide
  • DNA alkylating agent
  • Prevents protein synthesis -> anti mitotic activity.
  • Also has immunomodulatory effects. (selective for T cells)
  • Main indications are chemotherapy for haematologic malignancies (lymphomas, multiple myeloma), refractory nephrotic syndrome in children.
  • Solid organ cancers : breast cancer, disseminated neurobalstoma, retinoblastoma.
  • Side effects: haemorrhagic cystitis

Azathioprine


cyclosporine

  • Used in organ transplantation (kidney, pancreas, liver, heart), treatment of GVHD, and autoimmune diseases.
  • cyclosporine + MTX combination is standard treatment to prevent GVHD after allogenic stem cell transplantation.
  • Also used in rheumatoid arthritis, psoriasis.
  • Blocks activation of T cells
  • mechanism: Binds intracellular cyclophillin (a type of immunophillin) and the complex inhibits cytoplasmic phosphatase called calcineurin. Inhibition of calcineurin inhibits secretion of IL-2, IL-3 and IFN-gamma on antigen stimulated T cells.
  • It is a P450 substrate. -> requires individual dosage adjustments for patients
  • Side effects:
    • nephrotoxic, hyperkalemia,
    • seizures + altered mental status,
    • hirsiutism,
    • hypertension, hyperglycaemia
    • hyperplasia of gums (gingival)
    • Mnemonic: All Hs - hyperkalemia, (and kidney injury), hairy - hirsiutism, hyperglycemia, hypertension, hyperplasia of gums, "hysteria" - altered mental status
  • Tacrolimus is a similar agent with used for the same indications. However, it binds to immunophillin-FK-binding protein rather than cyclophillin.

[!TIP] Summary
Young patient with recurrent sinopulmonary infection, occular telangiectasia, cerebellar ataxia, and extrapyramidal motor function. They have increased risk of malignancy.

  • There is a mutation in the ATM gene.

[!INFO] This gene is necessary for the action of tumour suppressors p53 and BRCA1.

  • Classic AT usually presents either at birth, with partial combined immunodeficiency, or in early childhood, when neurologic abnormalities begin.
  • Immunodeficiency (there is reduced number of T cells) is most commonly manifested by recurrent sinopulmonary infections.
  • Progressive pulmonary disease and hematologic malignancy are major causes of morbidity and mortality
