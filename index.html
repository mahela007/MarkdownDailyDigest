DVT

Pathophysiology of DVT

Thrombosis = intravascular formation of a clot as opposed to haemostatic clot formation outside vessels.

Arterial Vs. Venous thrombosis

Arterial thrombosis -
Characterized by

  1. high sheer stress environment
  2. exposure of subendothelial tissue following plaque rupture
    Resulting in white thrombi - composed of platelets and fibrin.
    Main factors : turbulence and vessel wall dysfunction

Venous thrombosis -
Low sheer stress environment.
Therefore, red thrombi -> rich in red cells and fibrin.
main factors: Stasis and hypercoagulability.

DVT terminology

Distal - confined to calf veins
Proximal - extending into popliteal vein; larger and more like to embolize

Unusual site DVT: not originating in the deep veins of the leg.

[!INFO] ECG in pulmonary emblism
#ECG - Right ventricular strain pattern = T inversion in II,III,aVF and right chest leads V1-V4.

What is provoked and unproved DVT

DVT due to a transient risk factor => provoked DVT
Long term risk factors are "predisposing factors".

==provoked DVT means DVT in presence of transient risk factor==

Underlying diseases which cause DVT

Management of DVT

3 phases: Acute phase, maintenance phase and long term phase.

Acute phase

[!WARNING] Warfarin and DOACs cross the placenta
SHOULD NOT BE USED IN #pregnancy
DOC in pregnancy is LMWH = enoxa
If pregnant patient is already on warfarin, change to enoxa by the 6th week, when warfarin has high risk of causing embryopathy.

Traditional management:
  1. Parenteral anticoagulant + warfarin- provides immediate anticoagulation
    1. heparin or enoxaparin or fondaparinux + warfarin.

Parenteral therapy stopped once INR >= 2.0

Use of direct oral anticoagulants - newer regimens for acute treatment

Edoxaban and dabigatran - preceded by parenteral therapy for 5 days followed by a switch to DOAC.
Apixaban and rivaroxaban - no parenteral therapy required. But given for a higher dose for 7 to 21 days respectively.

Basis of pharmacotherapy

Anticoagulants prevent clot extension but do no dissolve formed clots.
but Thrombolytic drugs are not used in VTE because of their high bleeding risk. -> Can be given in life threatening situation.

Duration etc

3 months

Stopping anticoagulation before 3 months duration -> higher risk of recurrence. (5% within the next year)

Continuing treatment beyond 3 months doesn't have added benefit.

Haemostasis


How to read this diagram:

  1. The quickest response to endothelial damange is formation of the platelet plug. Platelets roll off the exposed collagen and stick to it.
    1. The platelets bind to collagen - adhesion
    2. secrete their granules - activation
    3. bind to each other - aggregation
  2. Platelets form the primary haemostatic plug within about 1 minute. This needs to be stabilized by the coagulation cascade proteins.
