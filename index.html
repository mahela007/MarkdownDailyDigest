Common stroke syndromes

Anterior circulation

Features of anterior circulation strokes / TIAs

1. Amaurosis Fugax - retinal artery embolus

Sudden vision loss in one eye. Caused by embolus in retinal arteries. Embolus probaby caused by internal carotid stenosis.

2. "limb shaking TIA"

TIA with involuntary limb movements - pathognomonic of severe carotid stenosis.

3. Aphasia
4. Hemiparesis
5. Hemisensory loss
6. hemianopic visual loss

ABCD^2 score estimates the stroke risk following TIA. > 6 -> high risk of stroke within 7 days.

Middle cerebral artery stroke (commonest) will cause a combination of the above

  • Hemiplegia
  • Hemisensory loss
  • homonymous hemianopia - optic radiations affected
    • Eye and head deviation towards affected side
  • Aphasia - dominant hemisphere involved.
  • Neglect syndromes - parietal lobe involvement
    Such a massive stroke carries a high risk of death due to coning from the oedema induced by infarction.

Internal carotid occlusion will produce a similar picture to MCA infarction but symptoms may be less due to collateral circulation.
