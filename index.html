GPA

[!TIP] Otitis, sinusitis, cough and glomerulonephritis and an older adult

GPA is the commonest ANCA associated vasculitis.
It is a granulomatous disease. The granulomas are not well formed like those in TB or sarcoidosis.
However, the granulomas can obstruct blood vessels and are necrotizing granulomas.

Lungs: Liquefactive necrosis seen in granulomas.
Kidneys: Focal necrotizing glomerulonephritis with crescents and thrombosis.

Affects older adults.
c-ANCA positive.

Upper respiratory tract : sinusitis , ears -> Otitis media.
Glomerulonephritis is present.

Diffuse alveolar haemorrhage and life threatening haemoptysis occurs in upto 45%.

Serum sickness

Self limited disease with excellent prognosis.
Occurs about a week after treatment with serum.
Occurs due to an antigen-antibody complex mediated type III hypersensitivity reaction.
Fever, rash (urticaria) and polyarthritis and lymphadenopathy are the main symptoms.
Symptoms resolve spontaneously after several weeks.
Pathophysiology: Patient starts to produce antibodies to a non-human proteins that was introduced into the system, usually via some type of treatment. The antibodies bind the foreign protein and form antigen-antibody complexes. If they are not cleared, they deposit in tissues and the synovial fluid.
Immune complexes may activate the complement pathway -> Low levels of C3 and C4 are found.
The process of clearing these deposited immune complexes causes the symmptoms.

Common offending agents

  • Vaccines
  • Antivenins
  • Monoclonal antibodies derived from animals
  • Streptokinase
  • Vaccines
  • (rituximab, infliximab)
