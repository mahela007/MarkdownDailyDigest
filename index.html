[!INFO] Summary: Measles

Mmnemonic: Incubation - 1 to two weeks; phase 1 - lasts 5 days (at most), phase 2 begins at 5 days.
Infective - 5 days before onset of rash.

  • Phase 1 : catarrhal phase : rhinorrhoea, cough, conjunctivitis, koplik's spots in the oral mucosa (lasts 2 - 4 days)
  • Phase 2: Eruptive / exanthematous stage : rash that spreads form the head outward. (fades away in the same order) (occurs 2 - 4 days after onset of fever)
  • infective 4 days before and 2 days after onset of rash.
  • Incubation period 8-14 days.
  • Droplet spread; highly infective.
  • No foetal abnormalities.

Occipital lobe strokes

Features:

  1. Homonymous visual field defects
  2. Central visual field defect
  3. Cortical blindness
  4. Alexis without agraphia (i.e patient can't read but can write because language isn't affected.
  5. Rarely prosopagnosia and visual hallucinations. (Charles Bonnet syndrome Source ) - hallucinations in a psychologically normal patient.

CRP

  • CRP was the first ⭐Pattern recognition receptor (PRR) to be identified.
  • Has proinflammatory and anti-inflammatory properties. Anti > pro.
  • Roles: Identifies and binds to foreign molecules and damaged local cells. ⭐Activates the classical [[#Complement system|complement]] pathway AND binds Fc receptors to facillitate phagocytosis - opsonin
  • CRP can be normal in SLE due to the production of interferons which inhibit hepatic CRP production.
