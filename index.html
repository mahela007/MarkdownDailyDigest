[!TIP] Summary

  1. Renal function is quantified by the GFR.
  2. GFR cannot be measured directly.
  3. However, since GFR is equal to clearance for substances which are neither secreted nor reabsorbed (by the tubules) in the STEADY STATE, we can use clearance to calculate the GFR. (See equation below)
  4. Inulin is one such ideal substance
  5. Inulin clearance gives an accurate calculation of GFR.
  6. But inulin is impractical to use in clinical practice.
  7. Therefore, we use an endogenous marker : creatinine.
  8. However, a little bit of creatinine is secreted by the tubules
  9. Therefore, creatinine clearance overcalculates GFR but since it correlates well with inulin clearance, we use this to calculate GFR.
  10. The theoretically correct way to calculate GFR (using inulin or creatinine) requires collection of 24 hour urine sample to measure 24 hour urine volume and average urine creatinine concentration. This is inconvenient
  11. Because of these limitations, equations have been developed to estimate GFR (eGFR) from the serum Creatinine concentration alone.
  12. These equations include the CKD-EPI, MDRD formula etc.
  13. However, these formulas provide inaccurate results in certain conditions; (fluid overload, low serum creatinine due to other causes etc. )
  14. See confounding factors below

Assessment of renal function

  • Effective renal plasma flow can be calculated using the Fick principle with para-amino hippuric acid.
  • PAH is filtered and secreted by the kidney (but not reabsorbed) -> i.e almost all of it is removed in a single pass through the kidney. (extraction ratio is around 90%).
  • This yields a normal renal plasma flow of 625 ml/min.
  • Renal blood flow is related to renal plasma flow by haematocrit.
  • If effective renal plasma flow is around 630, renal blood flow is 1273 mL/min.
    GFR in an adult male is 125 mL/min. It correlates well with surface area.
    GFR declines with age: 0.8 ml/min per 1.73 m2 per year after 40 years of age. Source

For such a substance, GFR is equal to clearance. Source
In practice creatinine is used because it is an endogenous substance. But creatinine is secreted in small amounts by the tubules (15% is secreted).
Therefore, using creatinine as the molecule slightly overestimates the GFR.
Accurately measuring creatinine clearance would require measuring urine creatinine.
So for convenience, we measure the serum creatinine and approximate the GFR based on that.
[!INFO] Confounding factors ^confounding-factors

  • Fluid overload causes dilution of Serum creatinine -> causes underestimation of GFR
  • Non steady state: The GFR equation above holds true only in the steady sate. Acutely changing serum creatinine will cause errors.
    1. AKI - eGFR will be inaccurate Source
    2. Pregnancy - Actual GFR changes due to physiologic hyperfiltration in T1 -> non steady state -> eGFR is inaccurate. Source
    3. Due to hyperfiltration, S.Cr decreases in T1, plateaus in T2 and rises back in T3. #pregnancy

Drugs causing cholestasis (DILI)

  • Drug induced liver injury is a recognized entity: DILI
  • However, it is generally rare. (UpToDate: about 10% of acute liver injury cases)
  • It is a diagnosis of exclusion. There are no confirmatory serologic tests.
  • Usually occurs within 3 months of drug use.
  • Resolution usually occurs with drug cessation.
    • Cholestatic injury takes longer to improve than hepatocellular injury.
