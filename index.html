Pathogenesis

Â It is virtually impossible to induce endocarditis in experimental animals unless the valvular endocardium is first traumatized with a polyethylene catheter inserted into the right or left side of the heart

Valve surface endothelium is normally resistant to microbial colonization. [[2023-SEMPaper#^staphAureus]]
but endocardial damage OR inflammatory endothelial lesion causes deposition of fibrin + platelet.
This produces platelet + fibrin clot
Initially sterile clot gets infected with microorganisms.
Source of bacteria : distal infection or Transient bacteraemia (which would usually be harmless)
Infection -> further activation of extrinsic clotting pathway.
Monocytes adhere to clot -> produce cytokines.
Activation of endothelial cells + platelets-> further deposition of fibronectin and more clot formation.
All this results in a macroscopic vegetation.

Bacterial growth occurs within cells and within the matrix of fibronectin inside vegetations, making it difficult for host immune responses to control or eradicate the ongoing infection

Which organisms like to attach to the heart valves?

  1. Staph aureus -> highly virulent -> Can attach to undamaged heart valves. ^staphAureus

Histology of vegetation

A mature vegetation is an amorphous collection of fibrin, platelets, leukocytes, red blood cell debris, and dense clusters of bacteria.
Vegetation can have very high concentrations of bacteria. (cos the immune system can't reach them)
Vegetations are avascular -> immune cells and antibiotics. can't reach bacteria.

Locations of vegetations:

Vegetations tend to develop at sites where blood travels from an area of high pressure through a narrow orifice into an area of lower pressure -? probably due to turbulent flow.

IE is commonest in aortic and mitral valves, except in IV drug users -> they get IE on the tricuspid valve.

Aetiological agents based on the different types of IE.

Native valve IE

Treatment duration 4 - 6 weeks

Left

Left sided vegetations tend to have higher bacterial load -> may need longer course of therapy.
Vancomycin is slowly bactericidal - may need longer treatment course.

Staph aureus - usually not penicillin sensitive; so penicillin is not appropriate.
Methicillin sensitive species -> can treat with semisynthetic penicillin like nafcillin, or oxacillin.
If Methycillin resistant -> treat with vancomycin.
Add on Gentamicin should not be used as risk of renal injury outweighs benefit of combination therapy.
Coagulase negative staphylococci (Staph. epiermidis) - Usually they have similar sensitivity patterns to coagulase positive staph. However, they are usually methicillin resistant; so treat with vancomycin.

Enterococci - IE causing enterocci are enterococcus faecalis.
They are resistant to many antibiotics. Vancomycin + gentamicin combination can be used because of synergistic action.

Gram -ve IE

Gram -ve IE usually coccurs due to HACEK organisms -> Virtually all HACEK organisms are highly susceptible to third-generation cephalosporins such as ceftriaxone, even strains that produce beta-lactamase.

IE due to other gram -ve organisms is very rare.

Right

Right sided IE = 10% of all IE.
Approximately 90 percent of patients with right-sided IE are people who inject drugs

More frequently in IV drug abuse ->
These patients are less compliant with long hospital admission -> faster acting antibiotics may be needed.

Staph aureus 70% >>> Strep 20% > enterococci 5%.
Treatment generally similar to left sided IE.

Prosthetic valve IE.

More difficult that native valve IE treatment. May require surgical replacement.
Staphylococcal infection requires combination therapy.
Minimum duration is 6 weeks.

Management options for IE

Surgical management

Early surgical management = surgery before completion of antibiotic therapy.
Indications - valve dysfunction causing heart failure, persistent infection, inctracardiac abscess

There is data to support the role for anti coagulants or anti-platelet medications to reduce the risk of thromboembolization in IE.

[!TIP] Overall picture
There is reversible airway obstruction in asthma.
It occurs in twophases:

  1. Early phase - Airway narrowing due to predominantly ASM mediated bronchoconstriction by mast cell released histamine + other mediators
  2. Late phase - More aiway narrowing due to further bronchoconstriction + local inflammation and oedema -> stimulated by inflammatory cells migrating to the mucosa during the late phase

variable narrowing of airway lumens causing variable reduction in airflow is pathognomonic of asthma.

Mechanisms of narrowing :

  1. Contraction of airway smooth muscle. <-mast cell and eosinophil mediators, such as leukotrienes C4, D4, and E4 and histamine, are potent bronchoconstrictors
  2. Thickening of the airway wall due to cells (inflammatory cell migration) or oedema
  3. plugging with mucus / debris
  4. airway remodelling

Bronchial hyper-responsiveness:
Another definingb feature of asthma = exagerrated reversible airway obstruction due to ASM contraction to physical, chemical or environmental stimuli. What causes airway smooth muscle to become hyper responsive to these stimuli is not known. Airway remodelling and altered function and mass of ASM could play a part.

Mast cell activation due to various other effectors is main factor in pathogenesis of asthma.
Mast cell activation by allergen serves as an example for what happens in asthma
4. But smooth muscle contraction isn't the only mechanism here since beta agonists don't completely reverse late phase bronchoconstriction.
1. In late phase, inflammatory cell migration, mucous plugging, oedema all contibute to further narrowing.

Innate immune system also plays a part in the pathogenesis of asthma. -> cells of the innate immune system are also found in excess in asthmatic airways.

Exhaled nitric oxide

Nitric oxide acts in the lungs to dilate both airways and blood vessels.
In asthma, epithelial cells produce NO probably to counteract the narrowing caused by other mediators.
Exhaled NO can be used as a marker of the degree of eosinophillic aiway inflammation. ->
i.e Exhaled NO increases in asthma.
