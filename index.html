[!INFO] Definition of DKA

pH can be normal while still statisfying criteria for DKA.

  • Two proposed reasons for observing lower glucose levels in DKA
    • Patients with DKA present earlier with symptoms. Symptoms of hyperosmolarity tend to present later. (so there's more time for hyperglycemia to develop)
    • Patients with DKA are younger and usually present early in the development of Diabetes. Young patients, and those with early diabetes have elevated GFR. Therefore, DKA patients usually have high GFR which causes a lot of glucose to be lost via the urine -> this limits serum glucose concentration.

[!INFO]
For DKA, expected target is resolution of ketonaemia and acidosis in 24 hours.

Replace total fluid deficit in 24 hours

Initial hour
- Second liter of fluid usually requires potassium addion. See [[#Electrolytes]] below.
60 minutes to 6 hours
  • Target a rate of fall of ketons of 0.5 mmol/L/hr or rise of HCO3 of 3mmol/L/hr.
  • Blood glucose fall of 3mmol / L / hr (54 mg/dL / Hr)
  • If blood flucose falls below 14mmol/L (252 mg/Ld) add 10% dextrose 125 ml/hr.
    Patients have a total potassium deficit (due to vomiting and diuresis) but it is masked by shift of KCl from ICF to ECF. Insulin administration will unmask the deficit and cause rapid fall of serum K+ as K+ shifts back into cells.
  • The addition of K+ is likely required with the second litre of fluid.
  • Potassium replacement doses:
    • Start potassium infusion even when K+ level is within normal

When to titrate dose:

  • If not meeting the targets [[#60 minutes to 6 hours|above]] above for rate of resolution
  • Can reduce dose to 0.05u/Kg/hr in patient becomes hypoglycaemic.

When to stop:
Until ketones < 0.6 mmol/L OR venous pH > 7.3 OR venous bicarb > 18 mmol/L.

Place for bicarbonate infusion

Use of bicarbonate is controversial. Some studies suggest it may impair renal ketone clearance and paradoxically worsen ketosis.
Few indications for bicarbonate infusion:

  • pH < 6.9
  • life threatening hyperkalemia because HCO3 will drive K+ into cells.
