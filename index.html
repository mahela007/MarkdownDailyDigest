Beta adrenergic agonists are the most potent and rapidly acting bronchodilator medications available for asthma treatment. Beta agonists interact with beta adrenergic receptors on a variety of cells to relax bronchial smooth muscle, decrease mast cell mediator release, inhibit neutrophil, eosinophil, and lymphocyte functional responses, increase mucociliary transport, and affect vascular tone and edema formation

The LABA formoterol has a rapid onset of action (similar to albuterol); combination inhalers containing glucocorticoid-formoterol can be used in some settings for relief of acute symptoms and for maintenance (off-label in the United States). LABAs should never be prescribed as monotherapy.

Tolerance to beta agonists can occur due to down regulation of beta receptors. This is problematic in patients who overuse their beta agonists instead of avoiding triggers.

Hepatocellular carcinoma

75 percent of primary liver tumors are HCC, with cholangiocarcinoma ≈ remaining 25%

The majority of HCC cases occur in the setting of chronic liver disease.
HCC is an aggressive tumour.

prognostic factors

  1. The severity of the underlying liver disease
  2. Tumor size
  3. Extension of the tumor into adjacent structures
  4. The presence or absence of metastases

Extrahepatic metastases occur as a component of disease recurrence after locoregional therapy in 5 to 25 percent of patients. 😓

Epidemiology

6th commonest cancer worldwide.
Male to by female : 3:1

[!INFO] Cirrhocis and chronic Hep infection is the main theme here
HCC Mainly occurs in background of chronic hep B infection or cirrhosis.

  • About 50% of cases are due to HBV infection and 20% due to chronic HCV.

    • Developing cirrhosis in background of chronic HBV infection massively increases risk.
    • Factors increasing risk of HCC in background of chornic HBV:
      • Presence of cirrhosis as above
      • HBeAg +ve
      • High viral load (high HBV DNA level)
      • High HBsAg
      • Viral coinfection with HCV or Hep D virus
    • Similar story for HCV -> HCC occurs almost exclusively in patient with chronic HCC who have developed cirrhocis. (i.e Cirrhosis due to HCV is a significant risk factor)
    • It is generally believed that HCC arises in the setting of rapid cellular turnover and the chronic inflammatory state induced by HCV

  • Chronic HDV is a risk factor for HCC.

  • Environmental risk factors:

    • Aflatoxin B1 - mycotoxin present in corn
    • Betel nut chewing!😱

  • Lifestyle

    • Alcohol - alcoholic cirrhosis; Alcohol can act synergistically with other coexisting HCC risk factors

  • Metabolic

    • NAFLD and NASH are risk factors

  • 30% of patients with cirrhosis of any cause will develop HCC.
    Non alcoholic fatty liver and NASH are also risk factors.

Protective factors against HCC

  • Antiviral therapy in chronic HBV or HCV reduces risk of HCC
    • Successful treatment of chronic HBV/C reduces but doesn't eliminate HCC risk.
  • Prevention : Hep B Vaccination
  • Drugs
    • Statins, Metformin, Aspirin and other NSAIDS are associated with lower risk of HCC.
  • Diet
    • Coffee intake may reduce HCC Risk.

Clinical manifestations of HCC

Decompensation of cirrhosis

Variceal bleeding / ascites due to portal obstruction.

paraneoplastic syndromes

  • Hypoglycemia<- metabolic demand of tumour
  • hypercalcaemia<- secretion of PTH related protein
  • Intractable diarrhoea
  • cutaneous features
  • erythrocytosis <- EPO secretion
    • usually masked because overall disease causes anaemia

Metastasis

The most common sites of extrahepatic metastases are
lung, intra-abdominal lymph nodes, bone [34], and adrenal gland, in that order.

Diagnosis of HCC

[!INFO] Diagnosis can be made by imaging alone

AFP is a glycoprotein that is normally produced during gestation by the fetal liver and yolk sac,
Alpha-Fetoproten level > 20 is a cutoff for further investigation. Sensitivity is only 60%. 😯
Specificity - 80%.
(So basically it increases the likelihood of the diagnosis )

AFP isn't suitable for screening: low sensitivity and specificity

Elevated AFP also occurs in

  • #pregnancy,
  • with tumors of gonadal origin (both germ cell and non-germ cell [51])
  • gastric cancer and other cancers.

Diagnosis can be made with dynamic contrast-enhanced computed tomography (CT) or magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) tailored for liver lesion evaluation.

Imaging diagnosis can obviate need for biopsy.

CT diagnosis criteria
  1. Size > 1 cm
  2. arterial phase hyperenhancement ⬆️ of center of lesion relative to the liver parenchyma
  3. In the portal venous or delayed phase
    1. Central hypoenhancement ⬇️ (relatively to rest of liver)
    2. Enhancing capsule seen
  4. Growth > 50% in 6 months

Overview of treatment HCC

Treatment options

Resection - curative in eligible patients; tumour should not show vascular invasion to be resectable.
Thermal ablation - similar eligibility criteria to resection, but resection generally preferred.

Hepatitis infection

Chronic infection = viral presence; doesn't mean continuous hepatitis. Hepatitis occurs in acute flare ups.

Hepatitis D infection

It's a defective virus which required HBV infection in order to infect the host.

Coinfection

HDV + HBV coninfection is clinically identical to acute HBV infection and usually transient and self limited. 😀
Higher risk of [[2023-SEMPaper#Acute Liver Failure|Acute liver failure]] 😕
Risk of chronic HDV infection is same as risk of HBV infection (because persistence of HDV infection is dependent on persistence of HBV.)

Superinfection

i.e patient has been a (diagnosed or undiagnosed) HBV carrier.
can present as severe acute hepatitis or exacerbation of chronic hepatitis.
Clinically we can't tell if it's superinfection or acute HBV but serology can distinguish the two.
Progression to chronic HDV occurs in all! 😣

Chronic HDV infection

Rapid progression towards cirrhosis.
Increased risk of hepatocellular carcinoma compared to monoinfection.

[!TIP] Cirrhosis and HCC are closely linked
So it makes sense that rapid progression to cirrhosis and increased risk of HCC occur in chronic HDV infection
