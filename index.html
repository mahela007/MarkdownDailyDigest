Discordance between ESR and CRP levels are seen in: (i.e low CRP and high ESR)

  1. SLE
  2. Ulcerative colitis but not Crohns disease
  3. Scleroderma
  4. dermatomyositis

In rheumatoid arthritis, the high CRP low ESR combination has been attributed to rapid CRP rise and slow ESR rise.

[!INFO] ESR and CRP in tuberculosis
None of the sources list TB as a causes of discordance between ESR and CRP. This is confirmed by

"CRP and ESR showed equal capabilities of monitoring response to therapy in multidrug-resistant-tuberculosis. None of them was superior to the other in monitoring response to therapy."
