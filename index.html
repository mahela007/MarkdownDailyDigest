Inheritance of mitochondrial diseases

A family history in which males and females are affected, affected females transmit the disease to ALL their children, and affected males do not transmit the disease to their children suggests mitochondrial inheritance
Mitochondrial disorders may present at any age (despite being inherited disorders)
Mitochondria produce energy. Mitochondrial disease are diseases which arise because of defects in the respiratory chain as 13 respiratory proteins are encoded by mitrochondrial DNA.
(Mitochondrial DNA encodes the RNA used for mitochondrial protein synthesis)
(The other proteins are coded for by nuclear DNA.)
Organs with greater dependence on aerobic respiration are affected more.
Each cell has many thousands of copies of the mitochondrial DNA and a certain threshold percentage of these must be affected for the disease to manifest.
So mitochondrial disorders could be caused by defects in mitochondrial DNA or defects in nuclear DNA. (but these "mitochondrial diseases" don't show "mitochondrial inheritance")

Mitochondrial disorders which occur due to defects in nuclear DNA have a wide spectrum of phenotypes.
The central nervous system findings are often fluctuating encephalopathy, seizures, dementia, migraine, stroke-like episodes, ataxia, and spasticity chorea.
Mitochondrial dysfunction should be considered in the differential diagnosis of any progressive multisystem disorder

  • ptosis, external ophthalmoplegia,
  • proximal myopathy and exercise intolerance, cardiomyopathy,
  • sensorineural deafness, optic atrophy, pigmentary retinopathy, and
  • Elevated fasting blood lactate level suggets mitochondrial disease.
  • A high incidence of mid- and late-pregnancy loss is also a common feature.
