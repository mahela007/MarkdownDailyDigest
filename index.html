[!TIP] Metoclopramide and domperidone: "Dream Big, Make it Count"

With respect to the BBB: Domperidone is blocked and Metoclopramide crosses

  • The side effects associated with metoclopramide include central side effects of anxiety, restlessness, and depression, hyperprolactinemia, and QT interval prolongation.
  • Long term use can cause irreversible tardive diskinesia. Source
    • Tardive Dyskinesia
  • These effects are not seen with domperidone because it doesn't cross the BBB.
  • However, domperidone does cause hyperprolactinemia. (Source: Katzung)
  • Metoclopramide is known to trigger phaeochromocytoma crisis.

Metoclopramide AND domperidone have dual action:

  • They block dopamine secretion peripherally at the GI smooth muscle;
  • Dopamine antagonizes acetylcholine.
  • So inhibition of dopamine causes increased activity of acetylcholine.
  • resulting in
    • increased oesophageal peristalsis
    • increased lower oesophageal sphincter done
    • but no effect on small intestine or colonic motility.

The basis of antiemetic medications

[!TIP] Mnemonics:
Muscarinic - M1 - Motion sickness
The emesis center is triggered by inputs from 4 sources:

  1. Chemoreceptor trigger zone in the base of the 4th ventricle
    1. It is outside the BBB and can sense ematogenic chemicals in the blood.
    2. The CTZ has dopamine and opioid receptors.
  2. Vestibular input: vestibular nerves input is mediated by muscarinic (M1) receptors and histamine receptors (H1).
  3. Vagal and spinal afferents from the GI tract:
    1. 5HT3 receptors stimulated by GI mucosal serotonin
  4. CNS : 'higher' inputs like emotion / disgust / stress etc.
