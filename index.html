In the normal brain, there is a balance between dopamine and acetylcholine. In parkinson's disease, the dopamine levels decrease, skewing the balance in favour of acetylcholine.This is why anticholinergic drugs can be used to treat the symptoms of Parkinson's disease.

[!INFO] L+B or L+C
levodopa + benserazie = cobeneldopa
Levodopa + carbidopa = cocareldopa

[!INFO] motor complications of treatment

  • End of dose wearing off effect
  • Choreiform movements / dyskinesias
    • They occur after 2 years of starting levodopa treatment. More with levodopa (compared to dopamine agonists) because levodopa has short half life and concentrations fluctuate.
  • On off phenomena
    • Also attributed to the short half life of levodopa.

[!INFO] Dopaminergic side effects of parkinson's treatment

  • Early side effects:
    • Nausea
      • Common Side effect of levodopa and dopamine agonists. Can be due to inadequate DDC inhibitor. Treat with domperidone but avoid metoclopramide which is a dopamine antagonist. Early side effect. Settles with time.
  • Late side effects:
    • Orthostatic hypotension
      • A late side effect. Managed by reducing drug doses.
    • Confusion / hallucinations
      • Another late side effect. Manage by reducing drug doses.
  • Impulse control disorders (ICDs)
    • Can occur at any stage of treatment.
    • Most commonly associated with dopamine agonists.
    • Severe manifestations include compulsive gambling and hypersexuality
    • Treatment is to stop DAs and if persistent, possible CBT.

[!INFO] Withdawal effects

  • Sudden withdrawal can cause parkinsonism-hyperpyrexia syndrome - basically, neuroleptic malignant syndrome occuring in the context of antiparkinson drug withdrawal or when switching antiparkinson's agents.
    • Levodopa or Dopamine agonists should not be stopped abruptly.
    • Abrupt Discontinuation of anticholinergics can cause acute exacerbation of Parkinsonism.
  • Dopamine agonist withdrawal syndrome:
    • Similar to cocaine or amphetamine withdrawal and include anxiety, panic attacks, depression, and sweating, nausea, pain, fatigue, dizziness, and drug craving. Only treatment is to restart the dopamine agonist.
