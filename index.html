Interncal capsule strokes

A short internal capsule overview
Internal capsule strokes are differentiated from cortical strokes by the absence of cortical deficits.
Cortical deficits such as seizures, aphasia, agnosia, and dysgraphia, apraxia, alexia, and amnesia.

Common stroke syndromes

Anterior circulation

TIAs

1. Amaurosis Fugax

Sudden vision loss in one eye. Caused by embolus in retinal arteries. Embolus probaby caused by internal carotid stenosis.

2. "limb shaking TIA"

TIA with involuntary limb movements - pathognomonic of severe carotid stenosis.

3. Aphasia
4. Hemiparesis
5. Hemisensory loss
6. hemianopic visual loss

ABCD^2 score estimates the stroke risk following TIA. > 6 -> high risk of stroke within 7 days.

Middle cerebral artery stroke will cause

  • Hemiplegia
  • Hemisensory loss
  • homonymous hemianopia - optic radiations affected
  • Eye deviation towards affected side
