Reticulocytes and assessment of erythropoiesis

Erythropoiesis

The first recognisable RBC precursor is the pronormoblast.
Once the nucleus is extruded, a reticulocyte results. They only have cytoplasmic RNA. No DNA.
Reticulocytes still have RNA so they can still synthesize haemoglobin. They circulate in the blood for 1-2 days before maturing and losing all RNA.

In intact erythropoiesis, reticulocyte count increases in proportion to the anaemia.
Erythropoiesis is stimulated by EPo which is produced by the peritubular, interstitial cells of the kidney in response to reduced oxygen delivery.
Timescale: after acute haemorrhage, EPO increases by 6 hours, retic count begins to increase in 2-3 days and peaks by 6-10 days and stays elevated until the Hb returns to normal.
Normal retic count - 0.5 - 2.5%

Mechanism of EPo

EPo increases the number of progenitor cells committed to erythrypoiesis.
In the chronic state, there is anatomical expansion of erythropoiesis into fatty marrow and even into extramedullar sites.

Hour 1 bundle

Sepsis is a medical emergency!
Upon recognition of sepsis:

  1. Measure lactate level
  2. Obtain blood cultures prior to antibiotics
  3. Give broad spectrum antibiotics
  4. Begin rapid administration of 30ml/Kg crystalloid for hypotension OR lactate > 4mmol/L.
  5. Apply vasopressors if hypotensive during or after fluid resuscitation to maintain MAP > 65mmHg.

Fluid:
Balanced crystalloid is better than normal saline. N/S caries a risk of hyperchloremic metabolic acidosis.

Vasopressors:

  • Central access is preferred but as line placement can cause delays, it's safe to give peripheral vasopressors for periods < 6 hours. (concerns are due to extravsation and local tissue injury).
  • Noradrenaline is recommended as the first line vasopressor.

IV corticosteroids:
Systemic corticosteroid accelerate resolution of shock.

SIRS score

SIRS is diagnosed by the presence of at least two of the following four SIRS criteria:

  1. body temperature > 38C or < 36C,
  2. heart rate > 90 beats/min,
  3. respiratory rate > 20 breaths/min or PaCO2< 32 mm Hg,
  4. leukocyte count over 12,000mm3 or under 4,000mm3 or immature band/neutrophil ratio exceeds 10%.
