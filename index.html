[!INFO] TIP: NAFL is not NALFD
NAFL is the mild end of NAFLD; NALFD is a collective term

  • Clinico-histologic diagnosis
  • Histologically looks like alcoholic liver disease but patient does not have a Hx of significant alcohol consumption.
    Commonest presentation is with incidental finding of abnormal liver enzymes or abnormal imaging. (mild elevation with ALT > AST, and also ⬆gamma-GT)
  • But 80% have normal liver enzymes. Source
  • Usually asymptomatic.
    NALFD is considered to be the hepatic component of metabolic syndrome.

[!INFO] Components of metabolic syndrome
H2CIL

Oxidative stress may then lead to lipid peroxidation.

  • Starts with fat accumulation in centrilobular areas and then spreads outwards. The liver will be soft, yellow and greasy. (i.e starts in ZONE 3)
  • Steatosis is macrovessicular.
    • Causes of microvessicular steatosis are different. (it's caused by mitochondrial dysfunction -> Reye syndrome; Aspirin is contraindicated in febrile children and teenagers)

[!INFO] The 4 "Dimensions" which will be reported in a liver biopsy sample sent to evaluate for the presence of NASH are

  1. Fibrosis (stage) <- the single strongest determinant of outcome.
  2. Steatosis
  3. Lobular inflammation
  4. Ballooning (3 and 4 constitute the grade)
  • Perisinusoidal scarring in space of disse also similary spreads outwards
  • The fibrosis reach the portal tracts and creates central-portal fibrous septa.
  • These changes cause micronodular cirrhosis. (aka Laennec cirrhosis) (< 0.3 cm in diameter)
