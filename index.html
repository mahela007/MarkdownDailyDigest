  • Eschar biopsy: lymphohistiocytic vasculitis.
  • Weil - Felix test - not sensitive or specific; is a heterophile antibody test based on shared antigens between ricketsia and proteus. Proteus antigens are used to test for typhus antigens. It should be carried out 5 - 7 days after onset of fever. slideshare presentation

medical nutrition therapy for hyperglycaemic in ward patients

  • The metabolic need for patients with diabetes is usually provided by 25 to 35 calories/kg where some critically ill patients might require less than 15 to 25 calories/kg per day.
  • Most patients will require 1,500-2000 calories (i.e kilocalories) per day with 12-15 grams of carbohydrates per meal.

[!INFO] What is a calorie?
The energy needed to raise the temperature of 1g of water by 1°C.
The 'food calorie' which is used to dietary calculations is actually a kilo calorie. (Energy needed to raise the temp of 1Kg of watery by 1°C)
