Features of HHS

  • Diagnosis:

    • Elevated LDH levels
    • Confirmation: of PCP requires BAL with silver stain as sputum often fails to show PCP.
    • Chest X ray: normal in early disease to diffuse bilateral shadows extending from perihilar region.Source
  • Then dark urine followed by pale stool and icterus. (i.e obstructive picture)
  • Initial response is IgM (which persists for about 3 months); Then class switching occurs to IgG which persists indefinitely and confers immunity.
