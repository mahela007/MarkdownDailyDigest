Myopathies

#2021BSQ-NOV/Q01

The typical presentation of a myopathy is:

Symmetrical
proximal muscle weakness, (i.e large muscles get inflamed more?)
wasting, (?inflmmation causes wasting somehow?)
normal sensation, and normal stretch reflexes. (neurology is intact)

Timeline of presentation

  • Acquired myopathies present in an acute or subacute fashion.
  • Inherited myopathies present with chronic symptoms (with the exception of metabolic myopathies) where symptoms can be acutely precipitated.

Causes of myopathy:

  • Inflammatory:
    • [[2021 General Medicine July#Polymyositis and dermatomyositis|dermatomyositis and polymyositis]], inclusion body myositis,
  • Endocrine:
    • Hypothyroidism, Cushing's
  • Electrolyte disorders
  • Metabolic myopathies:
    • carbohydrate, lipid, purine metabolic disorders
  • Drugs:
    • Cocaine, heroin, alcohol, glucocorticoids,
  • Infections - all sorts of viruses
  • Rhabodymyolysis - of various causes

Vasopressin like drugs

#2021BSQ-NOV/Q03
Vasopressin physiology:
3 classes of receptors for vasopressin:

  • V1a - Vasoconstriction
  • V1b - ACTH release
  • V2 (in basolateral membrane of DCT and collecting duct)- Antidiuretic response

Vasopressin antagonists:
Tolvaptan - Oral V2 antagonist -> causes free water loss
Conivaptan - IV antagonist of V2 and V1a.

Vasopressin agonists:

Vasopressin Desmopressin Terlipressin felypressin
Short duration Longer duration Longer duration Short acting
Weakly V2 selective V2 selective V1: mainly pressor action vasocontrictor

B12 deficiency

Glossitis + angular stomatitis + neuropathy
#2021SBR-JUL/Q09
Features of B12 defieciency neuropathy:

  • Neuropathic features are commoner in B12 deficiency than in B9 deficiency.
  • posterior columns first -> then lateral columns
  • Neuropathy is symmetrical, involves legs > arms and causes paraesthesia and gait problems.
  • The classic finding is subacute combined dengeneration of the spinal cord.
    • This leads to demyelination of the dorsal and lateral white matter. (i.e tracts).
    • Leading to sensory (paraesthesia, sensory ataxia) and motor (weakness, spacticity, paraplegia and incontinence).
