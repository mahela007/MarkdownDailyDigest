Mitral regurgitation

Basic Science

  • The apex beat is caused by ventricular systole.
  • Normally located in the 5th ICS, internal to the mid clavicular line.
  • Double apex beat is a feature of decreased ventricular compliance.
    • (hypertension, aortic stenosis, hypertrophic cardiomyopathy)
  • Pitch
    • high velocity of flow -> High pitch murmurs (MR)
    • low velocity flow -> low pitch murmurs (MS)

Aetiologies:

  • MR can be caused by LV dilation (which results from LV failure)
  1. Mitral valve prolapse
    1. The most common valvular abnormality (seen in 1-2% of people)
    2. Most are asymptomatic. Usually benign course.
    3. but complications:
      1. MR, infective endocarditis, sudden cardiac death.
    4. Associated with low body weight, thinly built people.
    5. Pathogenesis may have a inheritec component.
    6. Etiology: most often an isolated finding but associated with connective tissue disorders (Marfans, osteogenesis imperfecta) etc.
  2. Degenerative (age associated)
  3. Rheumatic heart disease
  4. Papillary muscle dysfunction (Ischemic or other)
  5. Cardiomyopathy (HOCM, dilated or restrictive)
  6. Connective tissue disease (Marfan's, [[2022 November SBR#Rheumatoid arthritis|rheumatoid arthritis]], ankylosing spondylitis)
  7. Congenital
    Signs:
    Signs of increased severity
  • small volume pulse
  • Enlarged LV
  • Loud S3
  • Soft S1
  • Early A2 - rapid ventricular decompression
  • Signs of PHT and left ventricular failure.

Murmur characteristics:

  • Location: 5th ICS, mid clavicular line or the location of the apex beat, radiates to the axilla
  • Duration: Pansystolic
  • High pitched (use diaphragm)
  • Soft S1
  • Can have associated S3.
  • S4 also may be heard.
  • Loudness correlates with severity.
  • Palpation:
    • Displaced apex beat
    • Parasternal heave (dilated LA displaces the RV)

Associations:

  • regurgitation -> atrial dilation -> atrial fibrillation is common. (LA is often larger in MR than in MS)
  • Sharp pulse upstroke due to rapid decompression of the ventricle.
