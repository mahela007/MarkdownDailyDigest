Hashimoto thyroiditis (chronic autoimmune thyroiditis)

De quervain (subacute thyroiditis)

#2021GM-JUL/Q23

  • AKA subacute granulomatous thyroiditis, subacute nonsuppurative thyroiditis.
  • Presumed to be caused by viral infection or post viral reaction.
  • Fundamentaly, a clinical diagnosis.
  • Neck pain + thyroid tenderness + diffuse goiter usually = subacute thyroiditis.
  • Acute thyroid damage -> release of T4 and T3 -> clinical and biochemical hyperthyroidism.
  • Histology: infiltration with neutrophils and lymphocytes.
  • Thyroid uptake scans will show reduced uptake during the hyperthyroid phase.
  • Treatment: For pain and systemic symptoms: Aspirin / NSAID, failing which prednisolone. Pain should subside in 1 to 2 days.
  • Recovery: The follicles regenerate and recovery is complete, through a transient hypothyroid stage, upto euthyroidism after a few months.

Pleural fluid analysis

#2021GM-JUL/Q30

Light's criteria

Any one of the following indicates that the effusion is exudative

  • Pleural fluid to serum protein ratio > 0.5;
  • Pleural fluid to LDH ratio > 0.6;
  • Pleural fluid LDH greater than 2/3 or upper normal limit;

Presumably, light criteria have been derived from observations of levels of LDH.

Pleural fluid tests

Elevated pleural LDH is seen in:

  1. 1000 IU/L (about x5 of ULN) -
    1. Empyema,
    2. rheumatoid pleurisy.
    3. Classically, LDH above 1000 itself indicated IC tube placement but this is not evidence backed.
  2. TB effusions and paraneumonic effusions - but levels are lower. (frequently > 500 in TB)
  3. Pleural to Serum LDH > 1.0 but pleural to serum protein < 0.5 is characteristic of pneumocystis jirovecii. (and urinothorax)

Pleural fluid glucose:

  • Usually similar to serum glucose.
  • Low in
    • Rheumatoid pleurisy
    • empyema
    • TB pleurisy
    • Oesophageal rupture
  • High pleural glucose is only caused by iatrogenic extravasation of some other fluid.

pH

[!TIP] Acidotic effusion -> exudate

  • Normal pleural pH is estimated to be 7.60.
  • Exudates are slightly more acidic (7.30 to 7.45) than transudates (7.4 to 7.55).
  • Inadvertent infusion of normal saline and urinothorax are the only 'transudates' that can have a pleural fluid pH <7.40
  • pH below 7.3 is seen in
    • Lupus pleuritis
    • rheumatoid effusion
    • Parapneumonic effusion and empyema
    • Malignant effusions

Adenosine deaminase:

[!INFO] What is adenosine deaminase?
Adenosine deaminase is involved in the purine salvage pathway and congenital deficiency of this enzyme leads to accumulation of toxic metabolytes which somehow predomimantly affect lymphocytes leading to the development of one type of severe combined immunodefficiency. (This variety SCID shows autosomal recessive inheritance). The upshot is that it's somehow involved in immune cells which may be why it's found in the effusion fluid in TB.

  • Commonest diagnostic test to establish a TB effusion.
  • cutoff > 40 IU/L (One uptodate article says 40-70 is 'indeterminate')
  • it has a high negative predictive value (TB is rare if ADA is < 40)
  • Generally considered to have high sensitivity and specificity.
  • 'False positives' can occur in empyema, mesothelioma, parapneumonic effusion.

Pleural fluid cell counts and differential

  • Normal pleural fluid contains mostly macrophages and lymphocytes. Total white cell count is < 2000 /microL

Posterior myocardial infarction (MI)

#2021GM-JUL/Q21

[!INFO] The facial nerve
Components of the nerve:

  • Motor fibers to facial muscles
  • Parasympathetic innervation lacrimal glands, submandibular glands and sublingual glands (but not the parotid gland?)
  • Afferent taste fibers from front 2/3 of the tongue.
  • Somatic afferents from the pinna and external auditory canal.

  • Bell's palsy is defined as 'idiopathic' isolated facial nerve palsy but a viral aetiology is suspected.
    • There is swelling with oedema and inflammatory infiltrate. Damage is greatest in the narrowest part of the passage: the labyrinthine part.
  • Accounts for 50% of peripheral facial nerve palsy with no age sex prediliction.
  • But there is a x3 risk during T3 of #pregnancy and week 1 post partum.

Basis: swelling of the nerve within the confined petrosal canal due to viral infection or reactivation. (HSV)
It is clinical diagnosis.
Symptoms: facial nerve weakness occurring over 1 - 2 days. Hyperacusis, loss of taste of anterior 2/3 of tongue, pain behind the ear.
facial sensation is intact.

Causes which need to be excluded:

  • Local pathology : cholesteotma, malignant otitis media.
  • Parotid tumours
  • Lyme disease can cause facial nerve palsy
  • HIV seroconversion is the most common cause in africa.
