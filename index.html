Examples

Benign ethnic neutropenia

Selective pressure by plasmodium vivax infection in Africa has caused reduced expression of the DARC receptor which leads to a small reduction in neutrophil count.

Severe congenital neutopenia

#autosomalDominant Kostman syndrome; appears in first year of life. Improves with G-CSF.

Clinical features:

Severe neutropenia is manifested by oral and pharyngeal infections, oral, cutaneous and anal ulcers, followed by sepsis.

Clinical utility of LDH

LDH is a cytoplasmic enzyme found in most tissues, but in different isozymes.

Therefore, LDH elevation is non specific.

Clinical utility is based on the context in which it's found to be elevated:

Elevation in Acutely ill patient Chronic elevation
Hypoxaemia / shock -> Tissue injury Chronic haemolysis
Acute, massive liver injury (eg. ischemic) Ineffective erythropoiesis
Life threatening syndreoms (SS, NMS) haematologic malignancies
Tumour lysis syndrome Used as a prognostic marker in NHL, prostate, MM, Testicular CA, germ cell CA (but not used for diagnosis)
Rapid intravascular haemolysis
Infectious like P. carinii, mono, malaria, TB, toxoplasmosis**** ****

LDH in pleural fluid: if pleural fluid LDH > 0.6 x plasma LDH (or > 2 ULN), pleural fluid is an exudate.
