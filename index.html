Trigeminal neuralgia

  • "neuralgia" not "cephalgia"
  • Classified as "facial pain".
  • Electric, shock like pain lasting seconds
  • Occurs in elderly, hypertension is the main risk factor.
  • Aberrant vascular loop causes compression of trigenimal nerve.
  • ?no autonomic trigeminal symptoms
  • Spontaneous remission occurs lasting months but disease invariably recurs
  • Treatment: Carbamazepine, surgical treatment is second line but more effective. (RF ablation)
  • Also, always think about [[2023-SEMPaper]] <- whipple's disease.

  • Extradrenal tumours (paragangliomas) produce mainly noradrenaline. Others produce both. [[2020-BasicSciences-July#Autonomic nervous system and organization]]
  • Unlike the healthy adrenal medulla, pheochromocytomas are not innervated, and catecholamine release is not precipitated by neural stimulation. The trigger for catecholamine release is unclear, but multiple mechanisms have been postulated, including direct pressure, medications, and changes in tumor blood flow.
  • Norepinephrine signals through α1, α2, and β1 receptors, while epinephrine will primarily stimulate only β1 and β2 receptors.

A very rare cause of seronegative arthritis and dementia.

  • Tropheryma whippeli - gram Positive Rod, PAS positive. Ubiquitous in environment.
  • Multisystemic disease; Migratory arthralgias precede other features by years.
  • CNS: cognitive impairment and dementia. (cerebellar ataxia, ophthalmoplegia.
  • GIT:
  • Endocarditis, pericarditis, myocarditis.
  • Typically affects white males, middle aged.
  • Suspect in RF negative migratory polyarthritis. (i.e Seronegative arthritis)
  • Extremely rare: only several hundred cases have been reported.
  • Untreated, poor prognosis.
  • Treatment: Initially penicillin or ceftriaxone to penetrate blood brain barrier followed by TMP-SMX for 12 months.
  • Diagnosis: Classicaly PAS positive macrophages on small bowel biopsy or PCR (from synovial fluid or recovered specimens) (?NOT on blood)
  • Good prognosis after treatment.

  • INR done within 20 hours has not prognostic benefit.

  • NAC and acetaminophen themselves can cause mild elevations of INR.

  • Activated charcoal is very effective. (1g/Kg given if patient presents within 4 hours of ingestion)
