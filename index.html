[!INFO] 1/3 rule (3rd letter of the alphabet) - Carcinoid

  • One third of these tumors are multiple
  • One third of those in the gastrointestinal (GI) tract are located in the small bowel
  • One third of patients have a second malignancy
  • One third of these tumors metastasize
- 95% originate in appendix, ileum and caecum. [Source](https://www.msdmanuals.com/professional/endocrine-and-metabolic-disorders/carcinoid-tumors/overview-of-carcinoid-tumors?query=Carcinoid%20Tumors%20and%20Carcinoid%20Syndrome)
  • They can be cancerous or benign.

The most common biologically active substance secreted from carcinoid tumors is 5-HT (serotonin), a vasoactive peptide whose biosynthesis is accomplished nearly exclusively by the enterochromaffin cells. Synthesized from the amino acid tryptophan, the release of 5-HT into the systemic circulation can cause the classic symptoms of the carcinoid syndrome, which include
1. diarrhea,
2. episodic flushing,
3. bronchoconstriction,
4. Hypotension
5. and eventual right-sided valvular heart disease (and probably oedema from right heart failure?)

  • Typical history: Woman of childbearing age who is overweight, presents with episodes of severe throbbing headache with papilloedema (bilateral and symmetric) and pulsatile tinnitus.
  • They can have visual disturbances and diplopia due to abducens nerve palsy.

⭐Phaeochromocytoma

  • Inheritance #autosomalDominant
  • Episodes of angiooedema not responding to anti-histamines or adrenaline, usually after a flu like prodrome.
  • Caused by C1 esterase deficiency.
  • No urticaria.
  • Deficiency of C1 esterase causes unchecked activation of C1, C2 and C4. (i.e classical pathway)
    • Serum C4 is the most reliable and widely used screening tool. Low levels of C2 and C4 are seen even between attacks.
    • C1-INH is low only during an attack
